Dear Members of the Board of the Division of Oil, Gas and Mining,
You have received many letters stating the FACTS and STATISTICS underscoring all the BAD REASONS the proposed
mine should be stopped from ever happening....
#1...COMPROMISED AIR QUALITY
#2...THREAT to our DROUGHT LEVEL WATER SUPPLY
#3...EXTREME RISK OF WILDFIRE
#4....DESTRUCTION of wildlife habitat, flora and fauna
#5....DESECRATION OF OUR WASATCH MOUNTAINS
#6... INDUSTRIALIZATION to the tune of 150 huge trucks a day hauling out the very
rock and earth that are the bedrock of this sacred corner of the world we call the Wasatch Front
I could go on and on but today I appeal to your hearts and souls to CONSIDER the consequences of allowing this
abomination to forever change the shape of Parley's Canyon!
As a fifth generation Mt. Aire Canyon girl, I feel as if I carry a voice for my ancestors who settled in the Salt Lake Valley
and then made Mt. Aire canyon their summer home!
I relished every minute of my childhood summers spent exploring every stream, nook and cranny of our little piece of
heaven with my sisters and cousins and Grandparents as my mother, EMMA LOU THAYNE (1924-2014) did!!. A poet,
writer, speaker, humanitarian (THAYNE CENTER FOR STUDENT LIFE, LEADERSHIP & COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT
@Salt Lake Community College)and teacher of English at the University of Utah, Emma Lou published her novel, NEVER
PAST THE GATE IN 1974 telling stories of adventures..."transcending either time or place" shared by a generation who
would head to their cabins (built in early 1900's) for the entire summer! Her novel rings true today as many of those
adventures are still to be had by our children and theirs not only in Mt.. Aire Canyon but in their passion for exploration in
ALL of the Wasatch Mountains!
My husband and I raised our 5 children through our love of the natural world by hiking, camping, skiing, water skiing,
biking and spending as much time at our Mt. Aire Cabin as possible! Other than spending 4 years in the Bay Area where
my husband did graduate work in Manual Therapy, we have lived in and LOVED our UTAH MOUNTAIN HOMES for
almost 50 years together! Our adult children represent this generation who make the great out of doors their LIFESTYLE,
not just a planned occasional hike or ski day, but a way of interfacing with the natural world that surrounds Salt Lake
valley as part of the very air they breathe! And that air is getting very polluted and would be even more compromised by
the working of a 650 acre open pit mine with 150 trucks a day spewing their exhaust into our already detrimentally
affected air!!!!
Our son, Jesse Rich runs to the edge of the threatened Grandeur Peak wilderness almost daily from his home at the
mouth of Millcreek canyon. As a nutritionist, trainer, new father of 2 adorable children and ultramarathoner, WINNING the
Wasatch 100 in 2019, he sees the threat to our very rights as explorers of the Wasatch mountains to be unconscionable!
Jesse's wedding reception was held at the INCREDIBLY BEAUTIFUL LOULAND FALLS which is part of the nearly
1,000 acres purchased by Jesse Lassley from Ira Sachs Sr. with the greedy intention to extract earth, gravel, minerals
and precious metals from a 650 acre portion (1/3 the size of KENNECOTT!) !! The exit for Louland falls is the same as
our Mt. Aire (Ranch Exit #132) which would be where approx. 150 gravel and cement trucks a day would be rolling
through!
Deceptively calling his company TREE FARMS LLC, Mr. Lassely nearly slipped under the radar filing for permits to have
begun tearing into the mountainside in 2021!!! Luckily an expose Article in the Salt Lake Tribune published last

November created a "landslide" of public interest fired up by people who are passionate about protecting our precious
natural resources!!!!!!! Why should our children and generations to come, along with the millions of visitors traveling
through scenic Parley's canyon have to suffer breathing bad air, hearing explosions,
seeing destruction, battling huge construction trucks constantly circling the lower canyon, while one company reaps
huge monetary gain!
So those of us who've taken exit #132 for now 67 years, and ALL who have cherished every nuance of each spring,
summer, autumn and winter in Parley's wild canyon, I SPEAK FOR STOPPING THE 650 acre GRAVEL PIT!!! Join
me @SaveParleys@gmail.com or SaveParleys.org .
