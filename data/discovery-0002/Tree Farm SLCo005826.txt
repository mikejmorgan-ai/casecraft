Dear Mr. Bradley,
Dear Council Members and Mayor Wilson,
As a sixth-generation Utahn and grandfather of six, all living within a few minutes of the Cottonwood Canyons,
I strongly support the recommendations of Salt Lake County’s Mountains Planning District and the Salt Lake
County Planning Commission to amend the Forestry and Recreation Zone and Foothills and Canyon Overlay
Zones.
I further support the revision of the Salt Lake County Ordinances to prohibit the uses of “mineral extraction and
processing; mine; quarry; gravel pit; including crushers or concrete batching plants used in connection with and
as part of an operation for the removal of sand, gravel and/or rock aggregate in the Forest Recreation Zone and in
the Foothills and Canyons Overlay Zone."
Mining and other extractive uses of the Wasatch’s land and water resources are inconsistent with the values of
our communities. They threaten our water, ecosystems and quality of life. I applaud the proposed ordinance
revision, which will speak loudly on behalf of all that depend upon and value the Wasatch Range.
Thank you for your consideration.
