Dear Mr. Snelgrove,
Dear Council Members and Mayor Wilson,
I applaud the fact that this mining proposal was reported on carefully for the public and exposed for what it is -a land grab with no consideration for the shared recreational use of the land as well as the health of those who
live in the valley. I have been a resident of Salt Lake City all of my life. Apparently, this LLC has been quietly in
the works for sometime as land was bought and sold until a hefty amount was acquired that would result in hefty
profits for those involved. This new proposed ordinance would make such projects less of a threat to those of us
who live in the valley. We must continue to protect the nearby mountains, lakes, and other environmental
resources that add so much to our daily lives. Few have such easy access to all that nature so generously
provides. We must reciprocate by protecting our natural resources from threatening enterprises.
I support the revision of Salt Lake County Ordinances to prohibit the uses of “mineral extraction and processing;
mine; quarry; gravel pit; including crushers or concrete batching plants used in connection with and as part of an
operation for the removal of sand, gravel and/or rock aggregate in the Forest Recreation Zone and in the
Foothills and Canyons Overlay Zone."
Thank you for your time and attention.
