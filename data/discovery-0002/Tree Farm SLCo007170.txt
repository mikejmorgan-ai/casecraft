Hello,
I am writing to voice my opposition to the limestone quarry mine a few miles east of our city on I-80. By now I’m sure
those involved are a little weary of reading letters so I will list my concerns in bullet points below:
1. The dust and diesel-fuel pollution and its impact on air quality in our neighborhood and our city.
2. The embarrassment to Utahns: A 634 acre eye sore will be created and seen when I-80 travelers drive through
Parley’s canyon. We already have scarring of our land at both the North Salt Lake area (the mining on the east side and
the refinery on the west) and also the south Point of the Mountain, the Kennecott mine and probably others. Must we also
have this scar in our beautiful canyon seen by so many travelers?
3. A nail in the coffin to our Outdoor Retailers trade show: REI, Patagonia, Northface and Kelty will ban the outdoor
retail trade show if it comes back to SLC and this project will be detrimental to getting the trade show and all of it’s
economic benefits back to SLC.
Thank you for your consideration and time in reading this letter.
