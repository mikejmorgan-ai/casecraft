Dear Ms. Carter,
I am addressing this letter of concern to alert the State of Utah regarding potential legal exposure from
allowing the Parleys Canyon open pit mine permit to advance forward.
The first area of concern comes from the enhanced risk to wildfires which might be generated from
excavating on a presently vegetated landscape at the proposed mine site. The trees and brush in all of
Parleys Canyon are seriously distressed from years of continuous drought. Two recent wildfires in
Parleys Canyon on August 6, 2020 and August 14, 2021, required the evacuation of hundreds of people
and the likely cost of millions of dollars in personnel and heavy air tanker support. The first fire was
blamed on a dragging chain behind a dump truck traveling east on Interstate 80 and the other one from a
disintegrating catalytic converter on a vehicle beyond Lambs Canyon. To the best of my knowledge,
neither vehicle or driver was located to assess the damages. I am sure that the State of Utah absorbed
some of these costs with their firefighting efforts on both fires. With such recent, proven causes for
wildfires in the Canyon, the risks from a mining operation with heavy equipment tearing up the hillside and
potentially hundreds of truck movements each day, only multiplies this exposure.
We only need to look at the wildfire near Boulder Colorado on December 30, 2021 to see the potential
risks to a cory,munity. This fire not only destroyed nearly1 ,000 homes, but also cost some lives.
Fortunately, neither the 2020 or 2021 wildfires in Parleys Canyon destroyed any structures or took lives,
but I think the result could have been vastly different had the wind be blowing in a different direction. We
were very lucky with both situations. Should a wildfire originate at this mine or along the frontage road
leading to Exit 132 in Parleys Canyon, the residents of Mt. Aire could be at great risk since there is only
one way in or out. The only other possible alternative is to drive to the end of the road and hike over the
ridge into Millcreek Canyon. This is not an attractive option for elderly people such as myself and there
are some who simply could not accomplish it. Yes, there might be loss of lives in addition to
structures. The State of Utah might be a party to any litigation if they proceed in the face of known risks.
Another area of concern for the residents of Salt Lake City is the exposure to their watershed of fugitive
dust from mining operations. This proposed mine is much closer to the Mountain Dell Reservoir than the
Harper pit. The wind frequently blows from west to east and would likely send this fugitive dust beyond
Mountain Dell Reservoir where it could coat the landscape feeding water into the reservoir. This dust
would also possibly contain hazardous properties which would need to be specifically removed at the
water treatment plant below the reservoir,
Please prevent this risky project from creating further RAPE OF THE LANDSCAPE in Parleys
Canyon. The claim by Tree Farm LLC that this mine would not be visible to the millions who travel on
Interstate 80 is a pure fabrication. It will be visible from either direction. This proposed mine only benefits
a few greedy individuals and could be an eyesore for future generations to come.
Thank you for your consideration of this matter.
