Dear Ms. Hepler,
I inserted the Latitude 40.734592N Longitude 111.944915W coordinates from the attached Google
Earth Pro map depicting the lower end of the proposed Parleys Canyon quarry into DNR's "Utah
Wildfire Risk Assessment Portal." The circled created on that Assessment Portal shows a 45 acre area
around the intersecting roads at the lower end (north) of the quarry property, (Attachment 3). Since
the actual property is ten times that size extending to the south, all of that parcel is rated from "High
to Very High to Extreme Wildfire Threat Level"
I can't see how the Division of Natural Resources can approve such a mining project given their own
published data on the dangers of wildfires in this vegetation stressed area. If this data is not right, then
DNR should update it, but considering the recent 2020 and 2021 wildfires in Parleys Canyon, it is
proven that it can burn. Should a wildfire originate from mining activities at this proposed site, then
the residents of Mt. Aire Canyon are in extreme danger from a wildfire cutting off their only drive out
route of escape and loss of life could occur.
Another major hazard can be illustrated from the daily prospect of 140 large ore hauling trucks
entering Interstate 80 west bound from Exit 132. This needs to be investigated by the Utah
Department of Transportation. If a slow moving truck starts the approach to the freeway on
Attachment 3, item "A" and enters the freeway at point "B" at a lower than freeway speeds, then
vehicles at point "C" whose drivers are blind to most of the entrance due to the curve in the freeway
are put in a perilous position. The drivers are forced to slam on their brakes or suddenly swerve into
the middle lane. Both of those actions are potentially dangerous. Also, the DOT needs to assess if the
structure of the Exit 132 overpass is capable of handling 140 heavy trucks per day over an extended
period of time without damage. The DOT also needs to explain how this mining operation can take
over and expand the frontage road to the quarry property from a one lane dirt access to a two lane
improved road without Salt Lake County FCOZ approval.
Attachment 4 illustrates the proposed quarry property in relationship of US Forest Service wildlands
and Mt. Aire Canyon private properties.
Please do the right thing and stop this project from proceeding for the safety of nearby residents and
the protection of our Wasatch Canyons beauty.
Respectfully yours,

~ 7+
Joseph Reimann,

Introduction
Welcome to the Utah Risk Assessment Portal (UtWRAP) and Assessment Report. The Internet
Portal is a component of the Utah Wildfire Risk Assessment Project (Utah WRA). This Report
provides two key pieces of information for the location you have selected. These are the
Wildfire Threat and the Characteristic (Expected) Fire Intensity expressed as the flame length.

Location
The information in this Assessment Report, Tree Farm LLC Wildfire Risk, is based on an area
that is approximately 45 acres in size centered at your location with the GPS coordinates of
40.7346° N 111.7449° W.

Wiltlfire Threat
Wildfire Threat is a measure of the likelihood of a fire starting and spreading to a location. The
Wildfire Threat Level map in Figure 1 is centered at your location. The colors on the map
shown in Figure 1 are based on the nine categories for the Wildfire Threat Level map with the
legend shown on the left side of Figure 2. The three Wildfire Threat Assess Groups are shown
on the right side of Figure 2 and are an aggregate of the nine Wildfire Threat Levels. The Assess
Group Wildfire Threat for your location is High. Collectively, areas with High Wildfire Threat
(High, Very High, Extreme Levels) are defined to support decision-making and occupy about
8.0% of the state. High Wildfire Threat is approximately 12 times greater than Moderate
Wildfire Threat and 53 times greater than Low Wildfire Threat.
