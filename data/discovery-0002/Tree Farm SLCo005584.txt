Councilman Bradley:
I support the revision of the Salt Lake County ordinances to prohibit activities of mineral extraction and processing and mining
of quarries or gravel pits in Forest Recreation Zone and the Foothills and Canyons Overlay Zone.

My strongest objections to such potential activities as proposed in Parley’s Canyon are concerns about air quality. We already
struggle with inversions, smoke from western forest fires, and winds whipping dust in the air in our dry climate. We’re facing
unknown additional air quality challenges from the Inland Port and potentially the further diminishment of the Great Salt Lake.
I can’t imagine how much worse it would get if the regular canyon winds coming down Parleys into Salt Lake were picking up
gravel dust from a mining or gravel pit operation there.

So much of the appeal of the Wasatch Front is the beauty and majesty of the mountains and their proximity for recreational
enjoyment. A mine or gravel pit in Parley’s Canyon would destroy this.

I hope you will vote to support the revised ordinances. Thank you for your time and consideration.
Beverly Hawkins
Salt Lake City
