Dear Mr. Snelgrove,
Dear Council Members and Mayor Wilson,
Adding a mine within the most densely populated area of our state that already struggles with poor air quality and
sensitive water resources is a reckless action jeopardizing the health and frankly, the economic future of our state. To this
end, I support the recommendations of Salt Lake County’s Mountains Planning District and the Salt Lake County Planning
Commission to amend the Forestry and Recreation Zone and Foothills and Canyon Overlay Zones.
I support the revision of Salt Lake County Ordinances to prohibit the uses of “mineral extraction and processing; mine;
quarry; gravel pit; including crushers or concrete batching plants used in connection with and as part of an operation for
the removal of sand, gravel and/or rock aggregate in the Forest Recreation Zone and in the Foothills and Canyons Overlay
Zone."
Further industrialization of the Wasatch’s land and water resources is inconsistent with the values of our communities. It
threatens our water, ecosystems and quality of life. I applaud this proposed revision of the ordinance that will speak
loudly on behalf of all that depend upon and value the Wasatch Range.
