Protecting the Wasatch Since 1972
Dear Mayor Wilson and members of the Salt Lake County Council,
On behalf of the Save Our Canyons community, I wanted to send a note of gratitude and thanks for taking a step
to help protect the Wasatch Mountains, our land, air, and water that are so important to our community, to our
wildlife populations and visitors to our wonderful county.
There is much more that needs to be done to help steward and protect our Wasatch Mountains. I hope we can
work with you, as a partner, to help put in place policies that protect these places before proposals confront the
Wasatch, damage the environment and the legacy we wish to leave behind. As we grow, pressure will continue to
increase - we need bold leadership to allow projects that are appropriate, that fit with our collective vision for the
future, but that prohibit projects that threaten, jeopardize or destroy our resources and the integrity of the
Wasatch.
Much work has been done, what the Wasatch needs is leadership from SLCo to pursue and implement much of
the hard work that has been done by those in your community over the past several decades - Mountain Accord,
Wasatch Canyons General Plan, Envision Utah, Water Quality Stewardship Plan, to name a few.
We all might differ slightly on how this is accomplished, but I hope that there is no question that the Wasatch
deserves protection. Let's get to work with that common belief, and work to build consensus on the how.
We believe saying thank you is as important as saying protect the Wasatch and Save Our Canyons - Thank you.
There is much work to do and I hope you can work to unite our communities around a vibrant vision for what the
Wasatch should be and follow through with meaningful actions as you did last night.
