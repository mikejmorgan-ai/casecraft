Dear Ms. Stringham,
Dear SLCo Mayor and Council,
Thank you, thank you for helping protect the Wasatch Mountains. There is so much pressure on these beloved
and critical places. With gondolas, road widening projects, mines, transportation projects, fuels build up,
unprecedented drought, growth and recreation pressures—there is much more that needs done to protect the
Wasatch. The Wasatch deserves protection.
Fortunately, between the Mountain Accord and the recently adopted Wasatch Canyons General Plan, there are a
lot of community vetted actions that the leadership of Salt Lake County can implement. As you well know, we
need comprehensive and dynamic policy to protect our watersheds and public lands. We need inclusive and
extensive recreation plans. We need integrated transportation solutions that help all users and visitors to our
canyons to leave their cars at home so that our wild and beautiful canyons are not converted into parking lots and
transportation contraptions. (Love/hate that verbiage: "transportation contraptions).
We look forward to working with you to do better in stewarding and protecting the Wasatch Mountains in Salt
Lake County, for the benefit of current and future generations, improving our quality of life and finding that
sweet spot, where man and nature exist in harmony and symbiosis with one another.
