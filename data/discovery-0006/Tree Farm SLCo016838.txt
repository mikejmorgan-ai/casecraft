Importance: Normal

We should not be allowing the extraction of gravel, minerals, etc in our canyons overlooking the Salt Lake Valley. First, it ruins the
beautiful view we have of the Wasatch mountains, but more importantly, it is hazardous to our health. This would create a traffic haxard
every day with more and more trucks entering and exiting, the mine area, and leaving behind a trail of rocks and other debris on our roads.
This is dangerous for other drivers on I-80. And, it will create dust and other pollutants in the air in Salt Lake Valley. We are already one o
the most polluted cities in the country, why should we encourage this sort of behavior? The Inland Port is already being built, adding to
more traffic and congestion. We certainly do not have a drop of extra water to spare, much less to use to keep the dust down from the mess
this will create.
I do not care if it means that the cost of concrete will be higher, or maybe might be lower. We have to think of the health o our citizens, and
our children. It impacts our quality of life. I urge you to change the ordinance to prohibit dangerous uses such as this. Even if they scale
down their proposal, this is not a use that is appropriate in our valley.
Thank you in advance for your vote to deny this use.
