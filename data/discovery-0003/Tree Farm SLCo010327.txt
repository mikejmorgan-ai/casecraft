Hi All,
I know that many of you were planning to give public comments today, and that some of you had to
miss the afternoon proceedings because of other commitments. I'm sending out this update in an
attempt to let everyone know what happened, and what to expect going forward. I'll start with the
minutes, and follow up with information about what to expect going forward. Please note that any
information given in this email is subject to correction and/or amendment, and that
corrections/amendments will be shared with this group.
Draft Meeting Minutes in the matter of Tree Farm, LLC
The Board began proceedings on the Tree Farm matter at approximately 1:00 p.m. The Board took
appearances of counsel, and the following parties identified themselves:
Kassidy J. Wallin, and Justin P. Matkin, Parr Brown Gee & Loveless, P.C., appeared in person on
behalf of the Petitioner, Tree Farm LLC;
Haley Sousa, and Charles A. Lyons, Assistant Attorney's General, Utah Attorney General’s
Office, Natural Resources Division, appeared in person on behalf of the Division of Oil, Gas and
Mining;
Janelle Eurick Bauer, of Jones Waldo Holbrook & McDonough, appeared in person on behalf of
Intervenor Save Our Canyons;
H. Michael Keller, Fabian Vancott, appeared in person on behalf of Intervenor City of Millcreek;
Steven J. Joffee, Michael Best & Friedrich, LLP, appeared electronically on behalf of Intervenor
Richards Family Partnership; and
Russell Vetter, Salt Lake City Attorney's office, appeared electronically on behalf of Intervenor
Salt Lake City.
The Chairman, Chris D. Hansen, announced that several members of the Board reside within the
boundaries of Salt Lake City or Millcreek City, and while none of them had expressed a desire to
recuse themselves, the parties were asked if they had any issues with Board Members living within the
boundaries of the Intervenors. None of the parties objected or expressed concerns and the
proceedings continued.
The Chairman announced that Tree Farm has filed a Request for Agency Action requesting a
declaratory ruling from the Board. The Board members have been reviewing the regulations
pertaining to declaratory rulings, but this is the first opportunity that the Board has had to consider this
matter as a quorum. The Board took a break to deliberate on this issue and determine the initial
procedures to follow going forward.
When the Board returned from deliberations, the Chairman announced that the Board had discussed
options as per R641-111 and they are ready to proceed with a vote on that rule. The Chairman, Chris
D. Hansen, entertained a motion on declaring a binding declaratory ruling. Alan Walker made the
motion, and LaVonne Garrison seconded the motion. The Board members, Chris D. Hansen,
Chairman; Richard K. Borden, Vice-Chairman; Stephen B. Church; LaVonne J. Garrison; Gordon L.
Moon; Selma Sierra; and Alan J. Walker, were present and they all voted affirmatively. Therefore, the
motion passed by unanimous vote.
The Chairman announced that the Board had discussed statute 40-8-7, and a related statute, 40-8-13,
which discusses the Boards' authority and the Divisions' authority. The Board believes that none of
the parties have adequately addressed that statute. Proceeding going forward, the parties should
provide a detailed brief on that statute, and associated statutes, and how it impacts this Request for
Agency Action.
The Chairman asked the parties to prepare supplemental briefings on 40-8-7 and associated statutes.
The briefings should be a maximum of 10 pages, and prepared within 14 days from the date of the
order, and to have simultaneous filings.

This matter is continued to the March Board hearing. The meeting adjourned at approximately 2:00
p.m.
Follow up/explanatory information
When is the March Hearing?
The Tree Farm matter is continued to the Board's regularly scheduled hearing on March 23, 2022,
which is slated to begin with a briefing session at 9:00 a.m., followed by the formal hearing at 10:00
a.m. A draft agenda will be forthcoming within the next few days, and the final agenda will be
published approximately one week in advance of the March 23 hearing.
The Board's Order regarding the Parties' Briefings:
The Board will issue an order regarding the briefings, and once that order is issued the identified
parties will have 14 days to prepare briefings. The Board has asked that the briefings be filed
simultaneously, which means that the briefings will be held in abeyance as privileged information until
all briefings are received, and then entered into the public record for review by the parties and the
public at the same time. I plan to use the usual method of sending a group email with all the briefings
attached therein.
When will the public be allowed to voice comments?
As I mentioned in a previous email, the Board's procedural rules dictate that the Board will first hear
from the Petitioner (Tree Farm), then from the Division, then from the identified Intervenors, Millcreek
City, Richards Family Trust, Salt Lake City Corporation, and Save Our Canyons. After the
aforementioned parties have concluded the testimony and evidence portion of the hearing, and before
the Board issues a ruling, the Chairman will open the meeting for comments by members of the public
who are interested in this matter. Although we did not make it very far into proceedings today, we did
make progress. At the March hearing, proceedings will continue, and provided that we make it
through the aforementioned procedures, public comment may be taken at the March hearing, or at a
subsequent hearing if the matter is continued. Please be assured that the Board is aware of
expressed desires to comment at the public meeting, and will allow time for comments as soon as
appropriate according to the Board's procedural rules.
