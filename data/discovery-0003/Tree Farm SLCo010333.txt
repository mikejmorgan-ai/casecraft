Pursuant to Utah Admin. Code R641-111-200, the Utah Board of Oil Gas and
Mining (“Board”) considered Tree Farm, LLC’s petition and deliberated on how to
proceed. Ultimately, the Board voted unanimously that a declaratory ruling
affecting the Petitioner, Division of Oil Gas and Mining (“Division”), and
Intervenors 1 is proper. Id at 230.
With respect to initial procedures under Utah Admin. Code R641-104, after
careful consideration of the filings submitted by the parties in the above-captioned
matter, the Board observed a gap in the briefing concerning how Utah Code Ann.
§ 40-8-7 should be interpreted, including but not limited to, how Utah Code Ann.
§ 40-8-7 fits into the overall structure of the Utah Mined Land Reclamation Act, and
the potential interplay between Utah Code Ann. §§ 40-8-7 and 40-8-13.

To establish a complete administrative record, and to inform itself of the
entire regulatory landscape surrounding this issue, the Board hereby ORDERS a
continuance of this matter to the next regularly scheduled Board meeting to be held
on March 23, 2022. Further, the Board hereby ORDERS the parties to submit
supplemental briefing specific to the respective powers and duties of the Division
and Board based on the relief requested, the stated positions in opposition and Utah
Code Ann §§ 40-8-7 and 40-8-13.
The Board further ORDERS supplemental briefing filed pursuant to this
Order must be filed within fourteen (14) calendar days of the issuance of this Order
and any submission shall not exceed ten (10) pages in length. Additionally, the
requested supplemental briefing is to be filed simultaneously, meaning the parties
shall file their supplemental briefing either on or before the deadline prescribed
herein.
