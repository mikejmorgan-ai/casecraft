Intervene; and (3) Save our Canyons Petition to Intervene; each of which was filed on January 25,
2022 (collectively, “Petitions”).
Utah Administrative Code R641-103-100(120) provides that a response to a petition to
intervene may be presented or filed at or before the hearing, but in the interests of justice and the

prompt resolution of these proceedings, Tree Farm files this Notice now so that the hearing
currently scheduled for February 23, 2022 may take place without further delay.
Pursuant to Utah Administrative Code R641-103-100(130) and (140), the Board may
decide whether to grant or deny the Petitions. The Petitions focus on the individual grounds for
standing. However, the Petitions do not raise any new arguments relating to the Division of Oil,
Gas, and Mining’s (the “Division”) justifications for denial of Tree Farm’s Notice of Intention to
Commence Small Mining Operations (S/035/0035) (the “NOI-SMO”). If the Board decides to
grant the Petitions, Tree Farm respectfully requests that the Board impose conditions or limitations
on the intervening parties’ participation in these proceedings that are necessary for the just, orderly,
and prompt administration of these proceedings. See R641-103-100(142). For example, the
intervening parties should not be allowed to present duplicative or repetitive arguments that have
already been presented by the Division or another party, and the intervening parties should not be
allowed to present arguments that are irrelevant to the NOI-SMO. See R641-103-100(145) (“In
the interest of expediting a hearing the Board may limit the extent of participation from an
intervenor. Where two or more intervenors have substantially like interests and positions, the
Board may at any time during the hearing limit the number of intervenors who will be permitted
to testify, cross-examine witnesses or make and argue motions and objections.”).

that judicial processes do not become “open forum[s] for resolution of political or ideological
