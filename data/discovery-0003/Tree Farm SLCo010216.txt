After the Request for Agency Action was filed in the above-captioned matter, several
municipalities, adjoining landowners and an environmental entity have moved to intervene. For
good cause appearing, The Board of Oil Gas and Mining (“Board”) hereby GRANTS the Petitions
to Intervene filed by the intervening parties.
Specifically, the following parties filed motions to intervene: the City of Millcreek
(“Millcreek”), Salt Lake City, Richards Family Partnership, and Save Our Canyons.
Tree Farm, LLC (“Tree Farm”), filed an Opposition to Millcreek’s Petition to Intervene on
January 26, 2022. Subsequently, Tree Farm filed a Notice of Non-Opposition indicating Tree Farm
continues to oppose Millcreek’s intervention but does not oppose the remaining parties’ Motions
to Intervene. The Division of Oil Gas and Mining (“Division”) has not filed an objection to the
intervention of Salt Lake City, Richards Family Partnership and Save Our Canyons. As a result,
Salt Lake City, Richards Family Partnership and Save Our Canyons’ Motions to Intervene are
GRANTED.

1

With respect to Millcreek, Tree Farm argues Millcreek does not have standing to intervene
in this matter. On February 10, 2022, Millcreek filed a Reply Memorandum in Support of the City
of Millcreek’s Petition to Intervene. The Division has not objected to Millcreek’s intervention.
Under Utah law, there are two tests used by the courts to establish standing, the traditional
test, and the alternative test. Utah Chapter of Sierra Club v. Utah Air Quality Bd., 2006 UT 74, ¶
18, 148 P.3d 960, 967. The traditional test or “distinct and palpable injury” test requires a party to
allege that the party has or will be injured by the challenged action; a causal relationship between
the challenged action and the injury exists, and the relief requested by the party is likely to redress
the injury. Utah Chapter of Sierra Club v. Utah Air Quality Bd., 2006 UT 74, ¶ 19, 148 P.3d 960,
967. The issues germane to this Order are satisfied by the traditional test, and thus the Board need
not discuss the alternative test.
After consideration of the points and authorities raised by Millcreek and Tree Farm, the
Board GRANTS Millcreek’s Petition. Millcreek has alleged potential injury to Millcreek and its
citizens from contemplated mining activities which may have a causal relationship with Tree
Farm’s challenged action. The relief Millcreek requests may prove likely to redress the injury for
which Millcreek seeks intervention. Further, granting intervention to Save Our Canyons, while
denying intervention to Millcreek, would be an inconsistent application of applicable standing
requirements.
Despite granting intervention to the above-referenced Intervenors, the Board remains
concerned with several of the issues raised in the filings, particularly issues raised that are outside
the Board’s jurisdiction and/or legislative mandates. The Board intends on addressing these
