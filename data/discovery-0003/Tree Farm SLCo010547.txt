Pursuant to Utah Admin. Code R641-104-100.160, R641-105-300 and Rule 41(a)(2) of
the Utah Rules of Civil Procedure, Tree Farm, LLC (“Tree Farm”) and Division of Oil, Gas and
Mining’s (the “Division”) jointly move the Board of Oil, Gas, and Mining (the “Board”) to
dismiss Tree Farm’s Request for Agency Action (the “Request”). Tree Farm and the Division
have met and conferred to discuss possible resolutions of the current procedural dispute over the
simultaneous filing by Tree Farm of a Notice of Intention to Commence Small Mining
Operations, S/035/0053 (“NOI-SMO”) and a Notice of Intention to Commence Large Mining
Operations, M/035/0054 (“NOI-LMO”). As a result of these discussions, Tree Farm and the

Division have stipulated and agreed as follows: (1) Tree Farm will no longer challenge the denial
of the NOI-SMO; (2) Tree Farm will withdraw its previously filed NOI-LMO; (3) Tree Farm
will refile a new notice of intention to commence small mining operations to be timely reviewed
by the Division; (4) Tree Farm will not file a notice of intention to commence large mining
operations simultaneously with the filing of the new notice of intention to commence small
mining operations; and (5) Tree Farm reserves the right to file a new notice of intention to
commence large mining operations at a future date, prior to enlarging small mining operations
beyond twenty acres, and with sufficient lead time to allow the Division to timely review the
new notice of intention to commence large mining operations.
Consequently, the Request is moot and a declaratory ruling is no longer required by the
Board as to the completeness of the NOI-SMO or the sufficiency of the proposed reclamation
surety. This stipulation is made specifically without prejudice as to the positions each of the
parties have taken in this Cause.
Although the intervening parties have not joined this motion, the arguments they raised
wholly relied upon, adopted, and/or mirrored the Division’s position. Therefore, Tree Farm and
the Division respectfully request that Tree Farm’s Request be dismissed, with each party bearing
its own costs.
