It turns out I had forgotten many of the important arguments. It is not that FCOZ or FR-20 specifically exclude
quarries and gravel pits, rather it is that the court agreed that 1) gravel pits are not a form of mineral extraction,
2) gravel pits are not compatible with the purposes of FCOZ and FR-20 Zones, and 3) that the county has a
ordinance that is specifically purposed for quarries, S-1-G Zone.

Additionally, the County's argument was that approval of the expansion of Harper's Quarry was appropriate
because it was an amendment of an older permit rather than an approval of a new permit. The County argued
that it would not approve a new permit for a gravel pit in the FCOZ and FR-20 zones. So if the County would
not have approved a permit for a new quarry in the FOCZ and FR-20 zones in 2001 is there a reason why the
County would approve such a permit in 2022? Maybe this is what we can discuss on Thursday morning.
