Thank you, Brian for being patient with our response! To answer some of your questions:
The proposed mine would require a conditional use permit/application under Salt Lake County Code Section 19.12.030(J)
and Chapter 19.84. The conditional use process would also include a review of the application for compliance with the
Salt Lake County Foothills and Canyon Overlay Zone Ordinance (County Code Chapter 19.72).
A conditional use application would be filed with the Greater Salt Lake Municipal Services District Planning Division. The
MSD can answer any specific questions regarding that process, including required grading permits, stormwater pollution
prevention plans, other agency reviews, etc. Maridene Alexander is their Communications Manager at 385-468-6733.
The following can be attributed to Mayor Wilson:
I am gravely concerned about this intent to mine over 600 acres in the heart of our Wasatch Mountains. Mining will
change the landscape, habitat, and health in Parleys Canyon. But it is also less than one mile from Grandeur Peak, and
thousands of hikers, bikers, and skiers safely recreating in Mill Creek Canyon. This is not in harmony with Salt Lake
County’s vision to protect the unique qualities of our canyons and public health for generations to come. Instead, the
development could potentially scar the natural contours of the landscape and could irreversibly disturb the experiences of
countless residents in these two canyons.
