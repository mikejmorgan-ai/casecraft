Pursuant to Utah Administrative Code§ R641-103-100, Richards Family Partnership
(“RFP”), by and through its undersigned counsel of record, respectfully moves the Board for
permission to file a Petition to Intervene in this matter out of time. For the reasons set forth
below, RFP submits that there is good cause for this motion, and that it should therefore be given
leave to file its Petition to Intervene, which has been contemporaneously filed with the Board.
FACTUAL BACKGROUND
1.

RFP is the record owner of five parcels of land located in unincorporated Salt

Lake County (the “RFP Property”) that are adjacent to or very near the property owned by Tree
Farm, LLC (“the Tree Farm Property”). The RFP Property consists of approximately 660 acres.
RFP also owns substantial water shares. See Declaration of Susan Trapp (“Trapp Declaration”),
1

attached as Exhibit “A” hereto, ¶ 6.
2.

RFP first learned that Tree Farm was looking to commence mining operations on

the Tree Farm Property after its acting manager read an article in the Salt Lake Tribune in late
November 2021. See id. ¶ 7.
3.

Shortly thereafter RFP began seeking out legal counsel with whom RFP could

consult regarding its legal rights in relation to Tree Farm’s proposed mining operations. Despite
diligent efforts, however, RFP was unable to retain legal counsel until January 21, 2022. See id.
¶¶ 8-9, 13.
4.

RFP received the Notice of Hearing on or about January 7, 2022. At that time

RFP’s manager did not notice the portion of the document stating objections must be filed by the
10th of January. In any event, however, RFP was continuing its efforts to obtain legal counsel.
See id. ¶ 12.
5.

It is important to RFP that it be allowed to intervene in this proceeding so that its

interests—and the interests of its partners—in this matter can be heard and considered. See id.
¶ 14.
6.

The RFP Property has been held by members of the Richards family (or entities

owned and controlled by family members) for more than a century. Any mining operations on
the Tree Farm Property will substantially impact RFP’s and its’ owners’ use and enjoyment of
the RFP Property. This impact will be felt by RFP in myriad ways, including, as mere
examples: (1) adverse impacts to the health, safety, and general welfare of its owners posed
by existing and the new proposed mining operations in Parleys Canyon, including fugitive
dust emanating from those operations; (2) significant increases in the use (and a fundamental
change in the nature of the use) of access roads leading to and from the RFP Property and the
Tree Farm Property; (3) noise pollution that will inevitably result from the proposed mining
2

operations; (4) substantial impacts on water supply and quality resulting from the proposed
mining operations; and (5) potential impacts on property value resulting from an adjacent
property owner operating what will inevitably become a large-scale mining operation. See id.
¶ 15.

7.

Additionally, RFP is concerned that Tree Farm is looking to commence mining

operations before its Large Mine NOI is fully considered and vetted. See id. ¶ 16.
ARGUMENT
R641-103-100 states: “Any petition to intervene or materials filed after the date a
response is due under R641-105-200 may be considered at the Board's next regularly scheduled
meeting only upon separate motion of the intervenor made at or before the hearing for good cause
shown.” Here, the Request for Agency Action was filed by Tree Farm on December 10, 2021,
and the Division’s response was due on January 10, 2022. See R641-105-200 (response due “by
the 10th day of the month or two weeks before the scheduled hearing, whichever is earlier, in the
month in which the hearing on the matter is scheduled (the "Response Date")”). Thus, the
deadline established under the Rules for filing a petition to intervene expired on January 10,
2022.
However, RFP respectfully submits that, for the foregoing reasons, there is “good cause”
to allow it to intervene out of time:
1. As soon as RFP learned of the proposed mining operations on the Tree Farm Property,
it undertook efforts to retain legal counsel to advise it regarding its rights and to
represent its interests.
2. For reasons outside of RFP’s control, RFP was unable to engage legal counsel until
January 21, 2022—eleven days after the deadline to intervene.

3

3. The issues presented in this proceeding involve complex legal and procedural matters
that RFP was not able to address without counsel.
4. Tree Farm, the Division, and proposed intervenor Millcreek City have agreed to
continue the hearing on this matter to February 23, 2022, and thus Tree Farm will
have ample opportunity to consider and respond to RFP’s Petition to Intervene.
5. Given RFP’s status as the record owner of property directly adjacent to the Tree Farm
Property, Tree Farm cannot in good faith contest RFP’s right to intervene and to have
its interests considered in this matter.
CONCLUSION
In light of the foregoing, RFP requests leave to file its Petition to Intervene out of time, so
that its interests may be heard and considered in this matter, which is of great consequence to its
present and future rights and interests in the RFP Property.
