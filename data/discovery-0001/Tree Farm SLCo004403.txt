Dear Mr. Bradshaw,
Dear Council Members and Mayor Wilson,
I've lived on the Wasatch front for the past 13+ years and have seen a great deal of change happen throughout my
time here, some changes that have driven lovely state forward and others that have come with severe set-backs.
After hearing about the potential mining proposed in Parley's Canyon, I knew I needed to voice my concerns.
Adding a mine within the most densely populated area of our state that already struggles with poor air quality
and sensitive water resources is an action that jeopardizes the health and the economic future of our state. With
this in mind, I support the recommendations of Salt Lake County’s Mountains Planning District and the Salt
Lake County Planning Commission to amend the Forestry and Recreation Zone and Foothills and Canyon
Overlay Zones.
I support the revision of Salt Lake County Ordinances to prohibit the uses of “mineral extraction and processing;
mine; quarry; gravel pit; including crushers or concrete batching plants used in connection with and as part of an
operation for the removal of sand, gravel and/or rock aggregate in the Forest Recreation Zone and in the
Foothills and Canyons Overlay Zone."
Further industrialization of the Wasatch’s land and water resources is inconsistent with the values of our
communities. It threatens our water, ecosystems and quality of life. I applaud this proposed revision of the
ordinance that will speak loudly on behalf of all that depend upon and value the Wasatch Range.
Thank you for your time, consideration, representation, and attention.
