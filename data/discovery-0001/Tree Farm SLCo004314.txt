Dear Mr. Bradshaw,
Dear Council Members and Mayor Wilson,
The Wasatch is a truly special place that we are so fortunate to have in our backyard and it's why I choose to call
Salt Lake City my home. I am gutted by the possibility of new mining operations taking place in an area that is
so important for ecosystems, recreation, and natural beauty.
I support the recommendations of Salt Lake County’s Mountains Planning District and the Salt Lake County
Planning Commission to amend the Forestry and Recreation Zone and Foothills and Canyon Overlay Zones and
the revision of Salt Lake County Ordinances to prohibit the uses of “mineral extraction and processing; mine;
quarry; gravel pit; including crushers or concrete batching plants used in connection with and as part of an
operation for the removal of sand, gravel and/or rock aggregate in the Forest Recreation Zone and in the
Foothills and Canyons Overlay Zone."
Our mountains are an invaluable resource and there is no going back if we choose not to protect them now.
