Working toward more inclusive & accessible RFAs
Introduction
The Diversity & Inclusion Office reached out to Contracts & Procurement last fall. Together, they convened a
workgroup with ORD, ODI, and IT to focus on reviewing RFA procedures and opportunities to improve
inclusivity, diversity, equity, and accessibility.
Challenges
• RFA process often challenging to navigate for community-based organizations
• The process is decentralized by design (a good thing!), but this impedes sharing lessons learned / best
practices across departments
• Room for improvement on diversity in RFA applicants, especially in recruiting new applicants
Workgroup’s objectives
• Create a community where people can seek help, share expertise, and test new strategies
• Share RFA best practices widely
• Introduce standards for documenting RFA experiences and lessons learned so that this knowledge lives
beyond the person who ran the RFA
• Maintain relationships with community partners, especially partnerships established during COVID
What we’ve done
• Collected common practices on RFA procedures across agencies under the Mayor’s Portfolio
o Developed RFA best practices slide deck
o Identified areas for learning and collaboration opportunities
• Assessed accessibility elements of applications and policies
o Introduced plain language reviews before RFAs posted (piloting with ORD)
o Evaluated ease of use for technical platforms
o Reviewed PDF compliance and taught people how to make PDFs accessible
• Created RFA Webex chat group to share current RFAs, questions, and examples (50+ people currently)
• Added more RFA guidance for county employees on the Contracts & Procurement website
o Added reaching out to Diversity & Inclusion Officer as part of RFA development process
• Developed dashboard with active RFA contracts to give visibility into partners who work with multiple
county agencies (in testing; not rolled out yet)
What we’re doing next
• Create a website with RFA opportunities that will link to the agency sites with RFA details
• Work with the DA’s Office to develop plain language explanations for dense legal sections in RFAs
• Get feedback from community partners through focus groups, interviews, and surveys
• Launch an ongoing internal survey to encourage reflection by RFA leads, document lessons learned,
and share within and across departments
What we need from you
• Support the initiative!
• Encourage anyone involved in RFAs to reach out and join the Webex group (contact Efren to join)
• Share your ideas to help us improve RFAs

E-mail Subject Naming Convention Guide
To strengthen the management in communicating with Mayor Wilson via e-mail, the Mayor’s Office has developed a naming
convention system for e-mail subject lines to be used when corresponding with the Mayor. The system is divided into two
categories: Informational and Actionable. All Scheduling or Event requests should go through the appropriate system and
should not be emailed.

INFORMATIONAL

Purely informational for the Mayor? If
so, use this subject:

INFORMATIONAL- {Statement of Topic}
Example: FYI – NO ACTION REQUIRED – NYTimes Article on COVID-19 Vaccines

ACTIONABLE

Action or response required? If so, use
this subject ine:

ACTION REQUIRED –DUE {Specify Date/Time – Statement of Topic}
Example: ACTION REQUIRED – Due October 1 by 2 pm – Signing/approving Chamber Letter
