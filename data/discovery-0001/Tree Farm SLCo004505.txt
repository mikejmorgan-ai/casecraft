Dear Mr. Bradley,
Dear Council Members and Mayor Wilson,
My name is Lee Moss. I am a resident of Salt Lake City and Salt Lake County and your constituent. I believe
that adding a mine in Parley’s Canyon within the most densely populated area of our state that already struggles
with poor air quality and sensitive water resources is a reckless action jeopardizing the health and frankly, the
economic future of our state. In addition, the noise from blasting and the permanent scarring of the landscape,
significantly degrade the recreational opportunities available. To this end, I support the recommendations of Salt
Lake County’s Mountains Planning District and the Salt Lake County Planning Commission to amend the
Forestry and Recreation Zone and Foothills and Canyon Overlay Zones.
I support the revision of Salt Lake County Ordinances to prohibit the uses of “mineral extraction and processing;
mine; quarry; gravel pit; including crushers or concrete batching plants used in connection with and as part of an
operation for the removal of sand, gravel and/or rock aggregate in the Forest Recreation Zone and in the
Foothills and Canyons Overlay Zone."
Further industrialization of the Wasatch’s land and water resources is inconsistent with the values of our
communities. It threatens our water, ecosystems and quality of life. I applaud this proposed revision of the
ordinance that will speak loudly on behalf of all that depend upon and value the Wasatch Range.
Thank you for your consideration.
