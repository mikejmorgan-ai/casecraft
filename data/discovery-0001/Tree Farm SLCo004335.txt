Dear Mr. Bradley,
Dear Council Members and Mayor Wilson,
I am writing as a member of Save Our Canyons and as a donor to Utah Physicians for a Healthy Environment.
From about 1989 to 1994, I was a member of the Salt Lake City Public Utilities Advisory Council. I was happy
to have co-drafted a report denying development of a ski resort in Lamb's Canyon.
I was concerned when a summer fire rampaged up I-80 and Lamb's Canyon that threatened to destroy my son's
Summit Park home.

Adding a mine within the most densely populated area of our state that already struggles with poor air quality
and sensitive water resources is a reckless action jeopardizing the health and frankly, the economic future of our
state.
To this end, I support the recommendations of Salt Lake County’s Mountains Planning District and the Salt Lake
County Planning Commission to amend the Forestry and Recreation Zone and Foothills and Canyon Overlay
Zones.
I support the revision of Salt Lake County Ordinances to prohibit the uses of “mineral extraction and processing;
mine; quarry; gravel pit; including crushers or concrete batching plants used in connection with and as part of an
operation for the removal of sand, gravel and/or rock aggregate in the Forest Recreation Zone and in the
Foothills and Canyons Overlay Zone."
Further industrialization of the Wasatch’s land and water resources is inconsistent with the values of our
communities.
It threatens our water, ecosystems and quality of life. I applaud this proposed revision of the ordinance that will
speak loudly on behalf of all that depend upon and value the Wasatch Range.
Thank you for your time and attention.
