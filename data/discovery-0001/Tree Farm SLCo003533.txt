5. Salt Lake City is the largest retail water provider in the State of Utah and has over 360,000
water customers. The City’s water assets include over 30,000 acres of property in the Wasatch
Mountain range that is designated watershed property. This includes approximately thousands of
acres of land owned by the City in Parleys Canyon. Parleys Canyon also contains the City’s
Parleys Water Treatment Plant, which processes up to 20 percent of the water used by Salt Lake
City water customers. The City maintains two reservoirs for drinking water and flood control in the
Parleys watershed.
6. The water rights in the Parleys Canyon area are administered by the Division, and the
primary guidance for relative share of the water and priority of water rights among the water users
was established by the Decree dated May 2, 1912, that adjudicated the Parleys Creek and waters by
Judge C. W. Morse. See Exhibit A. The Division commenced a modern adjudication for the
Parleys Creek Book (57-14) on October 16, 2017. The adjudication process allows all water users
to assert claims to water rights by filing a water user claim. After all water user claims are filed, the
Division identifies all “unclaimed” water rights to give water users another chance to assert claims
to use water in a drainage area. Through this process, the Division is able to evaluate the validity of
all rights in a particular drainage area.
7. The Division then issues and publishes a Proposed Determination (“PD”), which lists all
known water rights within defined boundaries of the book and identifies them as either “valid” or
“recommended to be forfeited.” The Parleys PD was published on March 1, 2022. See Exhibit B.
The PD also lists all water user claims that were filed by those asserting water interests in the
Parleys Creek sub-drainage.
8. Tree Farm LLC (“Tree Farm”) is listed in the Parleys PD as the owner of two primary
water rights. All primary awards are distributed on a pro-rata basis based on the flow of Parleys

described in Utah Code Section 73-3-3. This is a public application process with a formal
engineering review to make sure other water rights would not be impaired by the change and that
