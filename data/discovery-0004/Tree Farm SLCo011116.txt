Dear Mr. Baza:
Salt Lake County (“County”) has been monitoring Tree Farm’s recent Notices of Intent for
Small and Large Mines, which will be dismissed pursuant to a stipulation filed by Tree Farm
and the Utah Division of Oil, Gas and Mining (DOGM). During the March 23, 2022 Utah Board
of Oil, Gas and Mining meeting, Tree Farm represented that, following the dismissal of those
two NOIs, it intends to file a single Notice of Intent for a Small Mine. The County understands
that the NOI for a Small Mine would be an adjudicative proceeding “which shall commence
informally before the Division.” See Utah Administrative Code (UAC) R647-5-101(2). The
County further understands that the NOI for a Small Mine form constitutes a Request for
Agency Action, which commences an informal adjudication before the Division. See UAC R6475-104.1.12. Pursuant to the UAC, “each person known to have a direct interest in the
requested agency action,” may receive notice of an Agency Action by mail. See UAC R647-5104.2.14.
This letter constitutes the County’s formal request to receive notice of any NOI for a Small or
Large Mine that Tree Farm or other related entities file with DOGM, together with all
associated and subsequent filings in that matter. The County is a “person known to have a
direct interest in the requested agency action.” Id. This conclusion is based on the County’s
land use authority, the importance of which is acknowledged in Utah Code section 40-8-17(1),
which states, “[t]he approval of a notice of intention shall not relieve the operator from
responsibility to comply with all other applicable statutes, rules, regulations, and
ordinances…” See also UAC R647-1-102.3.
The County is the land use authority for unincorporated Salt Lake County. Tree Farm is
required to fully comply with the County’s land use and other ordinances before any
development or land use activity can take place on Tree Farm’s property. Accordingly, please
send notice of Tree Farm or related entity’s NOI for a Small or Large Mine and all associated
and subsequent filings to the following persons and addresses at Salt Lake County (at least via
email):
