DISCUSSION
RFP understands that the Board has requested the parties to provide briefing addressing
the interpretation of certain statutes and their impact on the issues the Board has been asked to
resolve in this matter. Because RFP believes that the other Intervenors can and will effectively
and comprehensively address those issues to the extent they remain relevant to the proceedings
before this Board, and to avoid unnecessary duplication of effort, RFP will not independently
1

address those issues but will rest upon and join in the briefing and arguments raised by the other
Intervenors.
Nevertheless, RFP will address one discrete issue that RFP believes is particularly
relevant to the concerns that RFP has raised with Tree Farm’s desire to immediately commence
mining operations. Specifically, Utah Code Ann. § 40-8-7(1)(i) grants broad discretion and
authority to both the Board and the Division to require “that mining operations be conducted to
minimize or prevent hazards to public health and safety.” That authority applies to both small
and large mining operations. Given the plethora of health and safety concerns that the proposed
mining operation poses—as addressed by RFP, the other Intervenors, and myriad public
commentators—it is entirely appropriate for the Board and the Division to require Tree Farm to
address those issues before Tree Farm begins mining operations.
It would be illogical to conclude that, although the legislature granted the Board and the
Division such broad authority with respect to ensuring public health and safety, the Board and
the Division cannot exercise that authority before mining operations commence. See Marion
Energy, Inc. v. KFJ Ranch P'ship, 2011 UT 50, ¶ 26, 267 P.3d 863, 869 (“Generally, when
interpreting statutes we seek to avoid interpretations "which render some part of a provision
nonsensical or absurd."); Monarrez v. Utah DOT, 2016 UT 10, ¶ 11, 368 P.3d 846, 852 (“we
avoid any interpretation which renders parts or words in a statute inoperative or superfluous in
order to give effect to every word of a statute.”) (cleaned up). RFP believes that the issues that
the Division has raised in its recently filed motion for regulatory guidance touch on these same
concerns.
Thus, whether or not the basis for the Division’s denial of the small mining notice was
2

technically proper, and even if Tree Farm is allowed to withdraw its pending applications, the
Division and the Board maintain broad discretion—which they should exercise under the
circumstances presented here—to ensure public health and safety concerns are addressed before
mining operations commence.
