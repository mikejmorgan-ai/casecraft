Intervenor Millcreek City (“Millcreek”) submits this supplemental brief in response to
the Combined Order Continuing Hearing and Requesting Supplemental Briefing of the Board of
Oil, Gas and Mining (“Board”) dated February 24, 2022 (“Combined Order”), ordering the
parties “to submit supplemental briefing specific to the respective powers and duties of the
Division and Board based on the relief requested, the stated positions in opposition and Utah
Code Ann §§ 40-8-7 and 40-8-13.” In its Combined Order, the Board stated that it “observed a
gap in the briefing concerning how Utah Code Ann. § 40-8-7 should be interpreted, including but
not limited to, how Utah Code Ann. § 40-8-7 fits into the overall structure of the Utah Mined
Land Reclamation Act, and the potential interplay between Utah Code Ann. §§ 40-8-7 and 40-813.”

On March 16, 2022, Tree Farm LLC (“Tree Farm”) and the Division of Oil, Gas and
Mining (“Division”) jointly moved the Board to dismiss Tree Farm’s Request for Agency
Action. Nonetheless, Millcreek files its supplemental briefing as requested by the Board’s
Combined Order. Additionally, Millcreek supports the Division’s Motion for Policy Guidance
filed March 17, 2022, which requests the Board issue policy guidance or regulation regarding the
issues raised by this matter. It is Millcreek’s position that this type of mining operation in such
close proximity to densely populated urban areas impacts the general welfare and presents issues
upon which policy direction from the Board is appropriate.
PROCEDURAL HISTORY
On November 12, 2021, Tree Farm LLC submitted to the Division a Notice of Intention
to Commence Small Mining Operations, Tree Farm LLC, Silver Mine, S/035/0053 (“Small Mine
NOI”) and a Notice of Intention to Commence Large Mining Operations, Tree Farm LLC, Silver
Mine, M/035/0054 (“Large Mine NOI”). The proposed mining operations have the same name –
“Silver Mine” – and the land affected by the Small Mine NOI is within the larger area of land
affected by the Large Mine NOI. By letter dated December 7, 2021 (the “Decision”), the
Division denied Tree Farm’s Small Mine NOI and explained that the “contemporaneous
submittal of these NOIs indicates Tree Farm LLC’s long-term intent is to commence operations
for a large mine,” and that in “the interest of administrative efficiency . . . [the Division] will turn
all focus and attention to the expedient review and processing of Tree Farm LLC’s Large Mine
NOI.”
MILLCREEK’S POSITION
In issuing its Decision, the Division correctly interpreted the Utah Mined Land
Reclamation Act (Utah Code Ann. §§ 40-8-1 et. seq., the “Act”) and the Board’s non-coal

mining regulations (Utah Admin. Code R 647). Tree Farm LLC should not be allowed to
commence mining operations under the Small Mine NOI, because Tree Farm’s intention is to
undertake a large mining operation under its Large Mine NOI which encompasses the land
affected by Tree Farm’s Small NOI and for which the Utah Mined Land Reclamation Act

Tree Farm’s proposed “Silver Mine” large mining operation encompasses Tree Farm’s
proposed “Silver Mine” small mining operation. In its Request for Agency Action, Tree Farm

circumvent the statutory and regulatory review process applicable to large mining operations by
seeking to commence mining operations immediately under the rubric of a Small Mine NOI
within the same area of its Large Mine NOI.
ANALYSIS OF UTAH MINED LAND RECLAMATION ACT
The plain meaning of Utah Code Ann. § 40-8-7 and 40-8-13 demonstrate that the
Division and Board have both the power and duty to require Tree Farm to follow the Large Mine
NOI process where Tree Farm simultaneously submitted a Small Mine and Large Mine NOI
encompassing the same mining area. Courts have “repeatedly affirmed [a] commitment to
interpreting statutes according to the ‘plain’ meaning of their text,” as that is the “best evidence
of the legislature’s intent.” S. Utah Valley Elec. Serv. Dist. v. Payson City, 2021 UT 68, ¶ 20,
502 P.3d 272, 276, reh’g denied (Jan. 6, 2022) (quotations omitted). However, courts “do not
interpret the ‘plain meaning’ of a statutory term in isolation,” but against the “relevant context of
the statute.” Id. at ¶ 26 (quotation omitted). The plain language of a statute is to be read “as a
whole and interpret[ed] . . . in harmony with other statutes in the same chapter and related
chapters.” State v. Harker, 2010 UT 56, ¶ 12, 240 P.3d 780, 784 (quotation omitted).

Utah Code Ann. § 40-8-7(1)(a) provides that the Board and Division “may require…that
a notice of intention for all mining operations be filed with, and approved by, the division,
before the mining operation commences pursuant to Sections 40-8-13 and 40-8-23.” (Emphasis
added.) Similarly, Utah Code Ann. § 40-8-7(1)(g), provides that the Board and Division “may
require…that with respect to all mining operations, a notice of intention is filed with and, if
required by this chapter, approved by the division before any such mining operations are
commenced or continued pursuant to Section 40-8-23.” (Emphasis added.) These provisions give
the Board and Division authority to require approval of a notice of intention before mining
operations commence pursuant to Utah Code Ann. § 40-8-13.
Utah Code Ann. § 40-8-13 addresses submission and procedures for review and approval
of notices of intention to commence mining operations. Section 40-8-13(1)(a) provides “Before
any operator begins mining operations. . . the operator shall file a notice of intention for each
individual mining operation with the division.” (Emphasis added.) Section 40-8-13(5) provides
“Except for the form and amount of surety, an approval of a notice of intention for small mining
operations is not required.” Section 40-8-13(6) provides “The notice of intention for mining
operations other than small mining operations [i.e., large mining operations], shall be reviewed
as provided in this Subsection (6).” Subsection (6), therefore, sets forth the procedures applicable
to the Division’s review and approval of notices of intention for large mining operations. The
plain language of Section 40-8-7(1)(a) and (g) makes it crystal clear that “for all mining
operations,” if approval of an NOI is required by the Act, mining operations may not be
commenced before such approval is granted. The plain language of Section 40-8-13(6) requires
an approval for Tree Farm to undertake the mining operations proposed under its Large Mine
NOI. The mining operations proposed under the Large Mine NOI include the mining operations

proposed under the Small Mine NOI. Therefore, the Division’s approval is required by the Act
“for all mining operations” proposed by Tree Farm and those mining operations may not
commence until an approval is granted.
Section 40-8-13(4)(b) is generally applicable to the Division’s review of notices of
intention (regardless of the size of the mining operations) and provides that “The division shall
notify the operator of any objections to the notice and shall grant the operator a reasonable
opportunity to take action that may be required to remove the objections or obtain a ruling
relative to the objections from the board.” The Division followed this procedure in denying Tree
Farm’s Small Mine NOI and advising Tree Farm of the Division’s intention to review Tree
Farm’s Large Mine NOI.
While the Act provides that an approval for a notice of intention for small mining
operations is not required (Section 40-8-7(5)), nothing in the Act precludes the Division from
objecting to a notice of intention for small mining operations under the circumstances presented
here. To allow Tree Farm to proceed to commence its proposed large mining operations under
the Small Mine NOI would directly conflict with the statutory scheme requiring that large
mining operations not commence prior to review and approval of a notice of intention for those
operations.
The Division’s interpretation of its authority to take this action is consistent with the plain
meaning of other terminology and provisions of the Act. The Act provides “[b]efore any operator
begins mining operations . . . the operator shall file a notice of intention for each individual
mining operation.” Utah Code § 40-8-13(1)(a). The use of the term notice of “intention” plainly
implies notice of what one intends to do. Under its nested NOIs and express statements in its
Request for Agency Action, Tree Farm clearly expressed its “intention” to undertake large

mining operations which encompass its “small mining operations” and are subject to the review
and approval procedures applicable to large mining operations under Section40-8-13.
Under 40-8-7(1)(c), “The board and division may require . . . for mining operations,
including small mining operations, the furnishing and maintenance of reasonable surety to
guarantee that the land affected is reclaimed according to approved plans consistent with onsite conditions.” (Emphasis added.) The term “land affected” used in Section 40-8-7(1)(c) is
defined in the Act as “the surface and subsurface of an area within the state where mining
operations are being or will be conducted . . .” Utah Code § 40-8-2(15)(a). (Emphasis
added.) Under Tree Farm’s nested NOI’s, the “land affected” must necessarily include the entire
area encompassed by its Large Mine NOI “where mining operations . . . will be conducted.” The
only way the Division can ensure that the surety will “guarantee that the land affected is
reclaimed” is to require surety covering reclamation of all the land affected by the Large Mine
NOI and accordingly, require that Tree Farm’s proposed operations be reviewed under the
statutory framework applicable to large mining operations. Moreover, this approach is also
consistent with the legislative finding in Section 40-8-2(3) that “[m]ined land should be
reclaimed so as to prevent conditions detrimental to the general safety and welfare of the citizens
of the state and to provide for the subsequent use of the lands affected” and with the authority of
the Board and Division under 40-8-7(1)(j) to require “that mining operations be conducted to
minimize or prevent hazards to public health and safety.”
CONCLUSION
The Division’s Decision is consistent with the plain text of the Act, which provides the
Division and Board with the power and authority to deny Tree Farm’s Small Mine NOI and
