County Planners:
Attached is a .docx file containing draft wording that you can use to incorporate critical mineral language into
your County Resource Management Plans within the mining and minerals section. I also included a map and
table showing where these minerals are located in Utah.
In February, the USGS released an updated critical minerals list for those minerals that are necessary for national
security and economic prosperity. The 2022 update removed uranium, helium, potash, rhenium, and strontium,
and added zinc and nickel.
The Critical Minerals of Utah Report (produced by the Utah Geological Survey) will be updated in a few months
to reflect these recent federal changes. The 2020 report still contains a lot of useful information that you can use
as you deem necessary on a county specific basis.
This critical minerals addition to the CRMPs does not need to be extensive, but I would recommend focusing
your efforts on adding in a few goals, objectives, and policies on the importance of critical minerals. The
attached Mining section from the SRMP contains goals, objectives, and policies that you can use or build on
based on your local needs.
