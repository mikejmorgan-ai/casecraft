**1.** **[SLCo010680]** **SUPPORTS**

**WHY CRITICAL:** This is a County Council public hearing presentation dated April 5, 2022, in which county staff explicitly stated that existing mining operations would be classified as legal nonconforming uses. This constitutes a direct admission by the county itself that existing mining rights predated and survived the zoning changes.

**RECOMMENDED USE:** Use as a county admission exhibit for Claim 3. County staff informed the Council that existing approved and operational mineral extraction would qualify as legal nonconforming uses. This establishes that the county was fully aware of, and publicly acknowledged, mining operations that predated the zoning changes.

**KEY QUOTE:** *"Existing approved, permitted, and operational mineral extraction/processing and similar uses would be legal nonconforming uses."*
