## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Tree Farm LLC seeks a **permanent injunction** to stop Salt Lake County from enforcing an ordinance that eliminates mineral extraction and processing as a conditional use in the Forestry and Recreation (FR) zone. The ordinance -- adopted April 5, 2022, amending SLC Code Sections 19.12.030, 19.72.190, and 19.76.030 -- is void as applied to Tree Farm because Tree Farm holds **vested mining rights** under Utah Code 17-81-401 to -403 (formerly 17-41-501 to -503).

The injunction claim is distinct from the declaratory judgment claim (Claim 1). Claim 1 asks the court to *declare* that Tree Farm has vested mining rights. Claim 2 asks the court to *stop the county from enforcing* the void ordinance against Tree Farm's operations -- including the business license denial (#COM21-1590), the refusal to process mining permits, and any future enforcement actions.

**Bottom Line:** The county engaged in a calculated sequence of enforcement actions -- denying Tree Farm's business license, eliminating the CUP pathway, and ignoring Tree Farm's vested rights -- that constitute ongoing, irreparable harm to Tree Farm's ability to exercise its statutory vested mining rights. A permanent injunction is the only adequate remedy.

## 2. CLAIM 2 VS. CLAIM 1: THE CRITICAL DISTINCTION

| Feature | Claim 1 (Declaratory Judgment) | Claim 2 (Permanent Injunction) |
|---------|-------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| **What it asks** | Declare Tree Farm has vested mining rights | Stop county from enforcing the void ordinance |
| **Legal mechanism** | Utah Declaratory Judgment Act | Equitable injunctive relief |
| **Focus** | Tree Farm's rights (affirmative) | County's enforcement actions (prohibitory) |
| **Relief** | Judicial declaration of rights | Court order prohibiting specific county actions |
| **Time orientation** | Establishes existing rights | Prevents future enforcement |
| **Standard** | Legal entitlement | Four-part equitable test |
| **Shared foundation** | Vested mining rights (17-81-401 to -403, formerly 17-41-501 to -503) | Same + enforcement evidence |

**Why both claims are necessary:** A declaratory judgment alone would establish that Tree Farm has vested rights, but would not compel the county to stop its ongoing enforcement pattern. The county could acknowledge the declaration but continue to obstruct Tree Farm's operations through administrative mechanisms (business license denials, permit refusals, code enforcement actions). The permanent injunction ensures the county must actually stop.

- **Primary Bates:** SLCo002501
- **Also appears as:** SLCo008382, SLCo012814, SLCo013323

- **Key quote:** "(b) has produced commercial quantities of a mineral deposit from the mining use."

## 5. THE CUP ADMINISTRATIVE TRAP

The county created a classic **administrative Catch-22** that independently supports injunctive relief:

### Step 1: County Required a CUP for Mining (Pre-Ordinance)
- Salt Lake County Code Section 19.84.020 provided: "A conditional use permit shall be required for all uses listed as conditional uses in the district regulations or elsewhere in this title."
- Mining (mineral extraction and processing) was listed as a **conditional use** in the FR zone under Section 19.12.030.
- **Bates:** SLCo002749 (Recommendation to Deny letter)

### Step 2: County Denied Business License Because No CUP
- On December 20, 2021, MSD Planning Manager Melissa Anderson recommended denying Tree Farm's business license because: "The MSD has confirmed that Tree Farm does not have a conditional use permit for the property that is the subject of this business license application."
- On December 27, 2021, Deputy Mayor Catherine Kanter formally denied Business License Application #COM21-1590.
- The denial letter cited SLC Code Section 5.02.120: "No license shall be issued for the conduct of any business... if the premises and building to be used for the purposes do not fully comply with the requirements of the county."
- **Bates:** SLCo002747 (denial letter), SLCo002749 (recommendation)

### Step 3: County Eliminated the CUP Pathway
- On April 5, 2022, the County Council adopted the ordinance **eliminating mineral extraction as a conditional use** in the FR zone.
- This means Tree Farm can never obtain the CUP the county said it needed.
- **Bates:** SLCo004662, SLCo018710

### The Trap:
1. **Before the ordinance:** "You need a CUP. You don't have one. Business license denied."
2. **After the ordinance:** "CUPs for mining no longer exist. Mining is now prohibited entirely."
3. **Result:** Tree Farm can never comply. The business license will never be granted. Mining cannot proceed.

This is the textbook definition of an administrative trap that requires injunctive relief. The county constructed a regulatory framework that makes compliance impossible -- precisely the type of ongoing, prospective harm that injunctions are designed to address.

## 8. STATUTORY FRAMEWORK -- VERBATIM LANGUAGE

### Utah Code 17-81-401 (formerly 17-41-501) -- Vested Mining Use

> **(1)** As used in this part:
> **(a)** "Mine operator" means a natural person, corporation, association, partnership, receiver, trustee, executor, administrator, guardian, fiduciary, agent, or other organization or representative, either public or private, including a successor, assign, subsidiary, related parent company, and predecessor in interest, that:
> **(i)** owns, controls, or manages a mining use; and
> **(ii)** has produced commercial quantities of a mineral deposit from the mining use.
>
> **(2)** A vested mining use:
> **(a)** **runs with the land**; and
> **(b)** may be changed to another mining use conducted within the scope of a legal nonconforming use or the permit for the vested mining use without losing its status as a vested mining use.
>
> **(3)** The present or future boundary described in a permit does not limit the scope of the mine operator's vested mining use and does not limit the protection provided under this chapter for a mining protection area.
>
> **(4)** A mine operator may provide notice of a vested mining use by recording a declaration of vested mining use with the recorder of the county in which the vested mining use is located.

*(Source: SLCo002501, SLCo003551)*

### Utah Code 17-81-402 (formerly 17-41-502) -- Expansion of Vested Mining Use

> **(1)** A mine operator may progress, extend, enlarge, grow, or expand the vested mining use to:
> **(a)** any surface or subsurface land or mineral estate that the mine operator owns or controls; and
> **(b)** any new land that:
> **(i)** is contiguous and related in mineralization to surface or subsurface land or a mineral estate that the mine operator already owns or controls;
> **(ii)** contains minerals that are part of the same mineral trend as the minerals that the mine operator already owns or controls; or
> **(iii)** is a geologic offshoot to surface or subsurface land or a mineral estate that the mine operator already owns or controls.

*(Source: SLCo003551)*

## 10. STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS

### 10.1 Lead with the CUP Trap
The CUP administrative trap is the single most compelling narrative for the court. It is easy to understand, clearly unfair, and demonstrates the need for injunctive relief:
- "The county told Tree Farm it needed a CUP. Then the county eliminated CUPs for mining. Now the county says Tree Farm cannot mine because it lacks a CUP that the county itself made impossible to obtain."

### 10.2 Use SG-5 as the Centerpiece
The staff report admission that the prohibition language was "not necessary in light of existing case law" is devastating because it comes from the county's own staff. It proves the county knew what it was doing was legally questionable and chose to proceed anyway with different wording that achieved the same result.

### 10.3 Emphasize the Recorded Declaration
The Supplemental Declaration of Vested Mining Use (SLCo003551) was recorded before the ordinance was adopted. The county had constructive notice. This is not a case where the property owner claimed vested rights after the fact -- Tree Farm recorded its declaration before the county even voted to initiate the ordinance proceedings.

### 10.4 Emphasize County's Refusal to Engage
Bateman made "multiple efforts" to engage the county. He sent letters, filed mediation requests with the Property Rights Ombudsman, and submitted public comments. The county gave "dismissive emails" and "largely ignored" him. This destroys any equity argument the county might make.

### 10.5 Frame the Injunction as Narrow
The injunction does not ask the court to invalidate the ordinance for everyone -- only to prohibit its enforcement against Tree Farm's vested mining operations. This is a narrow, property-specific remedy that respects the county's general zoning authority while protecting the specific property right at issue.

### 10.6 Statutory Structure Argument
Utah Code 17-81-403 (formerly 17-41-503) provides that a vested mining use can ONLY be abandoned by "recording a written declaration of abandonment." Tree Farm has not recorded any such declaration. Therefore, the vested mining use persists as a matter of law, regardless of the county's ordinance. The ordinance is simply void as applied.

### 10.7 Address CIM Exclusion Proactively
While this analysis deliberately excludes CIM/HB288/SB264 arguments, be aware that the discovery documents contain extensive references to CIM. The Bateman letters cite 17-41-402(6) and CIM provisions. If the court or opposing counsel raises CIM, note that Tree Farm's position does NOT depend on CIM classification -- the vested mining rights under 17-81-401 to -403 (formerly 17-41-501 to -503) are an independent statutory basis that applies regardless of whether the minerals qualify as CIM.
