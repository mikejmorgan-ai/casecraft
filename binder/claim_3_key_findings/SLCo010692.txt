Dear Board Members,
I am writing this letter in response to the Division’s letter for policy guidance. As an
Architect practicing in Utah and a concerned citizen i am requesting the Board to issue a
moratorium on Mining Permit’s in Class I and Class II counties of Utah until the board can
address the Division’s request for policy guidance.
In addition I ask the board to require applicants In Class I & II Counties to obtain approval
from the AHJ (authority having jurisdiction) prior to processing of applications for SMO
and LMO. Including if relevant the verification of any Vested Mining Rights. This will
prevent applications such as the proposed quarry along I-80 in Parley’s Canyon from
wasting the time and resources of tax payers in the state of Utah.
The issues exposed in the recent filings by Tree Farm LLC for a SMO, LMO and the
subsequent withdrawal from agency action has highlighted the need to update the policy
governing small mine applications in Class I and Class II counties. The current policy
regarding small mines has become outdated and does not address the current
development standards and priorities of the state of Utah.

Thank you for your consideration,
