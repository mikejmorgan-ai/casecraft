Dear Mr. Shaw:
Be advised that this law firm represents Tree Farm, LLC. It has come to my attention that Salt
Lake County is currently preparing ordinances to restrict the mining and extraction of critical
infrastructure materials from various properties my client owns in Parleys Canyon. The purpose
of my letter is to make you aware that any attempts to do so will be a violation of state law. In
addition, be aware that my client currently has a vested critical infrastructure materials operation
on the property.
Utah Code 17-41-402(6) reads:
(6)
(a)

(b)

A county, city, or town may not:
adopt, enact, or amend an existing land use regulation, ordinance, or
regulation that would prohibit, restrict, regulate, or otherwise limit critical
infrastructure materials operations, including vested critical infrastructure
materials operations as defined in Section 10-9a-901 or 17-27a-001; or
initiate proceedings to amend the county's, city's, or town's land use
ordinances as described in Subsection 10-9a-509(1)(a)(ii) or 17-27a508(1)(a)(ii).

Accordingly, under this section of the Utah State Code, Salt Lake County cannot enact or even
initiate proceedings to enact ordinances that restrict or prohibit critical infrastructure materials
operations. There are no qualifiers to this plain language. Thus, Salt Lake County cannot legally
adopt new ordinances that will restrict extraction of critical infrastructure materials on my client’s
property.
Moreover, the definition of vested critical infrastructure materials operation in Utah Code 10-9a901(4) reads thus:
