LEGAL AUTHORITY

Utah Code § 40-843 governs the process for the Division of Oil, Gas and Mining

(the "Division") to consider a notice of intention to commence small mining operations. It
provides as follows: "Within 30 days from the receipt of a notice of intention, the division shall
complete its review of the. notice and .shall make further inquiries., inspections, or examinations
that are necessary to properly evaluate the notice." Utah Code § 40-8-13(4)(a).
6.

If the Divisionlas any further inquiries or objections regarding the notice of

intention, "[t]he division shall notify the operator of any objections to the notice and shall grant
the operator a.reasonable opportunity to take action that may be required to remove the
objections or obtain a ruling relative. to the objections. from the board." Utah. Code..§ 40-813(4)(b) (emphasis added).
7.

"Except for the. form. and.amount of surety; an approval of a notice of intention. for

small mining operations is not required." Utah Code § 40-843(5) (emphasis added),
8.

The Division's rules further provide that a notice of intention to commence small

mining operations must be filed with the Division, and the Division must determine its
completeness before a mining operator may commence: operations. "Prior to commencement of
operations, a Notice of Intention to Commence Small Mining Operations (FORM MR-SMO)

2

containing all the required information must be filed with and determined complete by the
Division and the Division shall have approved the form and amount of reclamation surety. It is
recommended that the notice of intention be filed with the DiVision atleast thirty (30) days prior
to the planned commencement of operations." Utah Admin. Code R647-3.-101(1) (emphasis
added).
9.

"Within 15 days. after receipt of a Notice of Intention [to conduct small mining

operations], the Division will review the proposal and notify the operator in writing; [1] That the
notice of intention is complete and all required information has been submitted; or, [2] That the
notice of intention is incomplete, and additional information as identified by the Division will be.
required." Utah Admin, Code R647-3-101(2). "The Division will review and respond to any
subsequent. filings of information within 1Q working days of receipt:" Id.
10.

Importantly, neither the statutes nor the rules provide for an "approval"' or a

"denial" of a notice of intention to commence small mining operations. Rather, the process
conceived under the statutes and rules contemplates a process whereby an operator files a notice
of intention, the DiVision reviews it for completeness, and notifies the operator of anyportions of
the notice that are incomplete.
11.

The Division does, however, approve or disapprove of the form and amount of

reclamation surety. "The Division will review and approve or disapprove: The form and amount
of reclamation surety , . ,

Utah Admin. Code R647-3-101(3).

NOI-SMO and has failed to approve or disapprove of the form and amount of the proposed

ARGUMENT

The policy implemented by the State. of Utah is to allow small mining operations

to commence. once a completed notice of intention for such operations has been submitted. Utah
law does not contemplate an "approval" or "denial" by the DiVision as to a notice of intention.
Rather,. applicable Utah law provides for only .a determination by the Division of the
completeness of the notice of intention and a determination .as to the sufficiency of the

4

reclamation surety. Stated differently, there is no legal basis whatsoever for "denying" a properly
complete notice of intention to commence small mining operations, as the Denial Letter purports
to do, whether for "administrative efficiency" (as cited in the Denial Letter) or for any other
reason.
19.

The Division accepted the NOI-SMO and accepted the filing fee for the NOI-

SMO on November 12. Pursuant to. Utah Code § 40-8-13(4)(a), the Division had 30 days within
which to "complete its review of the notice and [to] make further inquiries, inspections, or
examinations that are necessary to properly evaluate the notice."
20.

The Division conducted such inquiries, inspections, or examinations as it deemed

necessary, and on November 18, issued a letter to Tree Farm noting all the deficiencies it was
able to identify with respect to the NOI-SMO. This was consistent with the process outlined in
Utah Code § 40-8-13(4)(b), which allows the Division to "notify the operator of any objections
to the notice and shall grant the operator a reasonable opportunity to take action that may be
required to remove the objections . . ."
21.

On November 24, Tree Farm responded to the Division's inquiries and provided

all information necessary for the Division to determine that the NOI-SMO was complete and that
the reclamation surety was sufficient. Tree Farm anticipated further correspondence from the
Division regarding the completeness of the NOI-SMO, consistent with the applicable statutes
and rules quoted above.
22.

But instead of determining the completeness of the NOI-SMO or the sufficiency

of the reclamation surety, the Division issued a "denial," which is not a legally cognizable
outcome.

5

23.

There is not adequate legal basis fora denial even if.a denial were allowed under

the rules. The Denial Letter does not identify any way in which the NOI-SMO is incomplete or
any way in which the proposed reclamation surety is insufficient. Rather, it states that because
Tree Farm filed two notices on the same day, the Division would simply disregard one of them.
'That is a violation of Tree Farm's legal rights. Tree Farm has a right,. under Utah law, to have its
NOT-.SMO reviewed.by the Division consistent with the applicable statutes and rules discussed
herein:
24.

Under Utah law, the Division may not act arbitrarilyin.issuing.its decision. See

Utah Code § 63G-4-403(4). Rather, the Division must decide all of the issues requiring
resolution, id. at -403(4)(c), it may not erroneously apply the law, id. at -403(4)(d), it must not
engagein an unlawful procedure or decision-making process, id. at -403(4)(e), it must follow
prescribed procedure, id. at -403(4)(e), it may not abuse its discretion, id. at -403(4)(h)(i), and it
may not act contrary to a rule, id. at -403(4)(h)(ii).
25.

Here,. the Division has failed to decide all of the issues. requiring resolution by

failing to give Tree Farm a decision as tothe completeness of the NOI-SMO and by failing to
give Tree Farm a decision as to the sufficiency of the reclamation surety.
26.

The Division has erroneously applied the.law by issuing a "denial" of the NOI-

SMO, which is not a legally cognizable outcome.
27.

The Division has engaged in an unlawful procedure by issuing a "denial" instead

of a determination of completeness.
28.

The Division has not followed prescribed procedure, outlined in the applicable

statutes and rules, which requires the Division to complete its review within a. prescribed time
frame; advise Tree.Farm of any items that are required to make the NOI-SMO complete, approve

6

or disapprove of the proposed reclamation surety, and ultimately issue a determination as to the
completeness of the NOI-SMO—not a "denial" or an "approval" of the NOI-SMO.
29.

The Division has abused its discretion by ignoring Tree Farm's duly-filed and

duly-accepted NOI-SMO, which Tree Farm is entitled under the law to have reviewed for
completeness, and instead, the Division has decided to "turn all focus and attention" to a separate
notice of intention. This is not an option given to the Division under Utah law and constitutes an
abuse of discretion.
30.

The Division has acted contrary to multiple statutes and rules, including Utah

Code § 40-8-13(4)(a), Utah Code § 40-8-13(4)(b), Utah Code § 40-8-13(5), Utah Admin. Code
R647-3-101(1), Utah Admin. Code R647-3-101(2), and Utah Admin. Code R647-3-101(3).
31.

The Division has acted contrary to its prior practice and has not provided a fair

and reasonable basis for the inconsistency. The NOI-SMO is not the same as the notice of
intention to commence large mining operations and must be considered on its own merit under
the statutes and rules applicable to the NOI-SMO. It is unfair and unreasonable for the Division
to simply decide that the two notices are one in the same, and refuse to consider the NOI-SMO.
While it is true that Tree Farm's long-term intent is to commence operations for a large mine,
Tree Farm's immediate intent is to commence operations for a small mine, and the Division has
illegally ignored that immediate intent and Tree Farm's legal rights under existing law. The
process for reviewing a small mine and large mine operation are separate and distinct inquiries
and the Division does not have the discretion to simply deny a small mine notice because a large
mine notice was also filed. Review of a small mine notice is not dependent upon the review of a
large mine notice and it was wrong for the Division to conflate these two separate processes.

Denial of Notice of Intention to Commence Small Mining Operations. Tree Farm

Dear Mr. Lassley:
On November 12, 2021, the Division of Oil, Gas and Mining ("Division") received both a Notice
of Intention to Commence Small Mining Operations ("Small Mine NOI") and a Notice of
Intention to Commence Large Mining Operations ("Large Mine NOI") from Tree Farm LLC.
The contemporaneous submittal of these NOIs indicates Tree Farm LLC's long-term intent is to
commence operations for a large mine.
Utah Code Ann. § 40-8-22(1) requires the Division to "cooperate with other state agencies, local
governmental bodies, agencies of the federal government, and appropriate private interest in the
furtherance of the purposes of this act." Based on a cursory review of the Large Mine NOI,
constituents from each of the stakeholder groups listed will likely need to be involved in the
permitting process.
Additionally, Utah Admin. Code R647-4-116 requires the Division to provide public notice of
tentative approval of Large Mine NOIs, a requirement that is absent regarding approvals of
Small Mine NOIs. This public participation, in conjunction with the cooperation required with
other regulatory bodies, is an important part of the permitting process for Large Mine NOIs.
In the interest of administrative efficiency and in the furtherance of the purposes of the Mined
Land Reclamation Act, the Division hereby denies Tree Farm LLC's Small Mine NOI and will
turn all focus and attention to the expedient review and processing of Tree Farm LLC's Large
Mine NOI.
