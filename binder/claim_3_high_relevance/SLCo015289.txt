Dear Mr. Williamson,

I’m writing to request that stream alteration application 22-57-05SA by Jesse Lassley’s Tree Farm, LLC
be denied by the State Engineer. As stated in the application, this is part of a larger gravel pit project
being proposed by Tree Farm that will require permits from the state’s Division of Oil, Gas, and Mining;
the Division of Air Quality; and Department of Transportation. It will also require a conditional use permit
from Salt Lake County. None of these permits have been approved. It will also require the establishment
of sufficient water rights for operations and dust mitigation which are currently in question.
At the very least, action on this request should be postponed and reconsidered after all of the permits
required for Mr. Lassley’s proposed 634-acre gravel pit have been approved. If the stream alternation
were to proceed, and the approvals for the gravel pit development are not granted, it will unreasonably
and unnecessarily affect stream’s recreational use, natural stream environment, aquatic and riparian
wildlife, and ability to convey high flows. And until the water rights issues have been clarified, the impact
of the proposed stream alteration on vested water rights cannot be determined.
