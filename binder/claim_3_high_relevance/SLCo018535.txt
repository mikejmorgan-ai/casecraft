Subject: Update on Tree Farm LLC Small Mining Operation
Dear Community Members,
I want to provide you with an update on this matter:
In speaking with Director John Baza, the Director of the Utah Division of Oil, Gas, and Mining (DOGM), I was
informed they denied the small mine permit application yesterday. If the decision is appealed, it will require a
public process, with public notice and public comment. The company has not yet appealed the denial to the
Board of Oil, Gas, and Mining, but they have indicated that such an appeal may be forthcoming in the next few
days.
For background, the small mine rules are not robust. For example, they do not take into consideration impacts to
this important corridor. In the past, a small mine was defined as 5 acres or less. Sometime in the early 2000s, the
Legislature enlarged the definition to 10 acres, and more recently, to up to twenty acres. Twenty acres is a
significant impact whether in an unincorporated area or not.
There are several next steps for how this might play out. The DOGM board could uphold the denial. If they
decided to reverse and approve the small mine, they do have rule making authority with power to change and
adopt rules. An additional concern if the small mine is approved by the board, is that the company could enlarge
their operations. In talking with Director Baza, it would then have to go through the process for a large mine
permit, which includes a total impact assessment. The concern is valid because it would be hard to get the genie
back into the bottle.
Things are moving quickly. I hope this provides a helpful update.
