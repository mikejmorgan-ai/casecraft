# VESTED MINING RIGHTS -- ATTORNEY BRIEF
## Tree Farm LLC v. Salt Lake County (Case No. 220903418)

**Prepared for:** Reviewing Attorney
**Strategy:** Vested mining rights is the PRIMARY claim. CIM preemption was a deliberate decoy.
**Key Statute:** Utah Code 17-41-501 to -503 (2021 Amendments, now 17-81-401 to -403)

---

## THE CASE IN ONE PARAGRAPH

Tree Farm holds vested mining rights on property in Parley's Canyon that has been continuously mined since the 1890s -- before Utah statehood. A large mine permit has been active since 1996. Hundreds of thousands of tons of minerals have been commercially produced. In November 2021, Tree Farm filed its Notice of Intention to commence new mining operations and recorded a formal Declaration of Vested Mining Use under Utah Code 17-41-501. Five months later, in April 2022, Salt Lake County amended its zoning code to ban all mining in the Forestry and Recreation zone -- explicitly in response to Tree Farm's NOI filing. Under the statute, Tree Farm's vested mining rights run with the land and cannot be terminated except by the owner's written declaration of abandonment. The County's ordinance cannot override them. No conditional use permit is required.

The County produced 5,576 documents. After scanning all of them, 40 scored as CRITICAL for the vested mining claim. Those 40 are duplicate productions of just **8 unique documents**. These 8 documents, read in sequence, prove every element of the vested mining rights claim.

### DOCUMENT 1: The Bill That Created VMU and CIM Protections
**H.B. 288 -- "Critical Infrastructure Materials" (2019 General Session)**
Bates: SLCo002501 (also SLCo008382, SLCo012814, SLCo013323)

**NOTE:** H.B. 288 is a bill, not a statute. It created the VMU and CIM statutory protections now codified at Utah Code 17-81-101 et seq. (formerly 17-41-101 et seq.). The relevant VMU statutes are 17-81-401, -402, and -403 (formerly 17-41-501, -502, and -503). H.B. 288 created CIM and VMU protections but did not create VMU itself -- vested mining rights predated the bill.

This is the enrolled text of H.B. 288, which codified the legal framework Tree Farm relies on. It establishes two parallel protections:

**Vested Mining Use** (Utah Code 17-81-101 (formerly 17-41-101(26))): A mining use by a "mine operator" that existed before the County restricts mining. Requires that, as of January 1, 2009, the operator (a) owns or manages a mining use under a large mine permit issued by DOGM, and (b) has produced commercial quantities of a mineral deposit. A vested mining use:

- **"runs with the land"** (17-81-402(2), formerly 17-41-502(2))
- **"is conclusively presumed to be a use consistent with the highest priority use"** (17-81-402(3)(a), formerly 17-41-502(3)(a))
- **cannot become a nuisance** if operating for more than three years (17-81-403(2), formerly 17-41-503(2))
- can only be abandoned by **"a written declaration of abandonment"** by the owner (17-81-402(5), formerly 17-41-502(5))

**Why it matters:** Tree Farm meets every statutory element. Large mine permit since 1996. Commercial production of 725,000+ tons in 2009 alone. No written abandonment. The County cannot override these rights with a zoning amendment.

### DOCUMENT 2: The Recorded Declaration of Vested Mining Use
**Supplemental Declaration and Notice of Vested Mining Use**
Bates: SLCo003377 (also SLCo009126, SLCo013723)
Recorded: November 12, 2021 | Entry No. 13822822, Book 11268, Pages 4405-4413

This is the formal recorded instrument filed by Tree Farm under 17-41-501. It declares:

> *"A mining use... existed or was conducted or was otherwise engaged in on the Property by Portland Cement Company of Utah... **at least as early as the 1890s, before statehood**, and well before Salt Lake County restricted or otherwise limited mining use in the area."*

The declaration establishes the chain of title through Portland Cement Company of Utah (later Utah Portland Quarries, Inc.), Harper Contracting, Pharaohs Glen Land Company, P.G.L.C., and finally Tree Farm, LLC. It documents:

- **Large mine permit approved by DOGM in 1996** -- remains active
- **Commercial production:** 90,000+ tons (1994), 725,000+ tons (2009), 386,000+ tons (2019)
- **Expansion rights** under 17-41-502 to "any surface or subsurface land or mineral estate that the mine operator owns or controls"
- Property includes patented mining claims: Shale and Limestone Placer (Mineral Survey No. 5031), Compromise Placer, Union Limestone Quarry Placer, Portland Cement Nos. 1 and 2

> *"the present or future boundary described in the Large Mine Permit **does not limit the scope of the Declarant's vested mining rights** and does not limit the protection provided under Utah law for a mining protection area."*

**Why it matters:** This is the single most important document. It was recorded on November 12, 2021 -- five months before the County's April 2022 ban. It satisfies every statutory requirement: mining since the 1890s, large mine permit since 1996, commercial quantities produced, formal declaration recorded. The chain of title is documented through every predecessor entity.

### DOCUMENT 3: Tree Farm's NOI Filing
**Request for Agency Action -- NOI-SMO (S/035/0053)**
Bates: SLCo003551 (also SLCo003560, SLCo003569, SLCo003602, SLCo003611, SLCo003699, SLCo010728, SLCo010795, SLCo010804, SLCo010813, SLCo015369, SLCo015414, SLCo015423)
Filed: December 10, 2021

Tree Farm's Request for Agency Action before the Board of Oil, Gas and Mining, seeking a declaratory ruling that its Small Mine NOI is complete. The filing documents the timeline:

- **November 12, 2021:** Tree Farm submitted NOI-SMO and paid $150 filing fee
- **November 18, 2021:** Division said NOI was "incomplete," requested additional information
- **November 24, 2021:** Tree Farm responded to all deficiency items
- **December 7, 2021:** Division issued unprecedented "Denial"

Tree Farm's legal argument: *"neither the statutes nor the rules provide for an 'approval' or a 'denial' of a notice of intention to commence small mining operations... there is no legal basis whatsoever for 'denying' a properly complete notice of intention."*

The NOI-SMO exhibits show:
- **Mine Name:** "Silver Mine"
- **Minerals:** Hard rock sand and gravel, limestone, cement powder, precious metals
- **Operator:** Granite Construction (national company)
- **Pre-mining land use listed as:** "Mineral Mining" (confirming property already used for mining)
- **Reclamation bond:** $186,000 (demonstrating commercial seriousness)

**Why it matters:** Tree Farm was actively pursuing mining operations months before the County's ban. The property's pre-existing land use was already "Mineral Mining." The $186,000 bond and engagement of Granite Construction show this was a real commercial operation, not a paper claim.

### DOCUMENT 4: The County's Ban
**Salt Lake County Council Agenda Package -- Ordinance Amending Section 19.12.030**
Bates: SLCo005003 (also SLCo011775, SLCo016381, SLCo017826, SLCo004662, SLCo011435, SLCo016040, SLCo017485, SLCo018710)
County Council Meeting: April 5, 2022

This is the County's legislative package to amend its zoning code to **eliminate mineral extraction as a conditional use and explicitly ban mining** in the Forestry and Recreation zone. The staff report is explicit about why:

> *"the recent Notices of Intention to Commence Small and Large Mining Operations, **filed by Tree Farm, LLC** with the Utah Division of Oil, Gas, and Mining for certain property in Parley's Canyon, raised the question of whether mineral extraction and processing should continue to be a conditional use"*

The ordinance adds: *"The following uses are explicitly prohibited in this chapter: mineral extraction and processing; mining."*

The County included a carve-out for "property within the FR zone that currently has approved, permitted, and operational mineral extraction/processing" -- but this is narrower than the statutory protection, which protects uses that "existed or was conducted or otherwise engaged in" before the prohibition.

**Why it matters:** The County's own staff report admits the ban was triggered by Tree Farm's NOI filings. This is the "political subdivision prohibits, restricts, or otherwise limits a mining use" event that activates the vested mining rights protections under 17-41-101(26). The ban came five months after Tree Farm's Declaration of Vested Mining Use was recorded. The County's carve-out attempts to limit protection to "approved, permitted, and operational" uses -- ignoring that the statute protects uses that merely "existed or was conducted" before the prohibition.

### DOCUMENT 5: Tree Farm's Formal Assertion of Vested Rights
**Sur-Reply to the Division's Response (Board of Oil, Gas and Mining)**
Bates: SLCo006947 (also SLCo006963, SLCo010271, SLCo010287)
Filed: February 18, 2022 | Author: Kassidy J. Wallin, Parr Brown Gee & Loveless

This is Tree Farm's earliest formal assertion in the regulatory record that it holds vested mining rights:

> *"**a conditional use permit from Salt Lake County will not be required on this site because Tree Farm holds a vested mining use.** The Division's Response to Tree Farm's Reply clarifies that the Division did not point to County zoning or any other government permit 'as a basis for denying the Small Mine NOI.'"*

The filing also cites R647-3-114, which "expressly contemplates 'mine enlargement' and requires an operator to file a notice of intention to commence large mining operations if the operator intends to expand beyond 20 acres." This regulatory framework anticipates exactly what Tree Farm is doing -- starting small and expanding under its vested rights.

**Why it matters:** Tree Farm put its vested mining rights claim on the record before the County's April 2022 ban. This was not an after-the-fact argument manufactured for litigation. Tree Farm's attorney asserted the vested rights claim two months before the County acted.

### DOCUMENT 6: The Division Takes No Position on Vested Rights
**Division's Response to Petitioner's Reply (Board of Oil, Gas and Mining)**
Bates: SLCo006843 (also SLCo006857, SLCo010158, SLCo010172)
Filed: February 10, 2022 | Authors: Haley Sousa and Charles A. Lyons, Assistant AGs

The state Division of Oil, Gas and Mining explicitly declines to challenge Tree Farm's vested mining rights:

> *"**Petitioner's Claims Regarding Vested Mining Use are Outside the Scope of the Board's Jurisdiction.** ...The Division takes no position regarding Petitioner's vested mining use because neither the Division nor the Board have jurisdiction to make a ruling on that issue."*

The Division also makes a critical admission: *"the Division has never in its history denied a complete Notice of Intention for Small Mining Operations."*

**Why it matters:** The state's own mining regulatory agency does not contest Tree Farm's vested mining rights. It says only that the Board lacks jurisdiction to rule on them -- leaving the question for the courts. If even DOGM won't challenge the vested rights claim, the County's position is weaker. The admission that the Division has never before denied a complete Small Mine NOI underscores that Tree Farm was treated differently.

### DOCUMENT 7: The Division Identifies the Conditional Use Requirement
**Division's Response in Opposition to Request for Agency Action**
Bates: SLCo006684 (also SLCo009809, SLCo009924)
Filed: January 10, 2022 | Authors: Haley Sousa and Charles A. Lyons, Assistant AGs

The Division's opposition brief lists the permits Tree Farm would need:

> *"Petitioner will need several permits in order to mine under the Large Mine NOI, including but not limited to, water discharge permits, a fugitive dust permit, a storage tank permit, **a conditional use permit**, possibly a takings permit, among others."*

The Division also states: *"extraction and processing should continue to be a conditional use in the Forestry and Recreation (FR) zone."*

**Why it matters:** The Division identifies the conditional use permit as a requirement -- the exact permit that Tree Farm argues is unnecessary because of its vested mining rights. If Tree Farm's vested rights are valid, the County's conditional use requirement is superseded by state law. This document frames the central legal question: does the vested mining use statute eliminate the need for a County conditional use permit?

## THE NARRATIVE: HOW THESE 8 DOCUMENTS BUILD THE CASE

**Step 1 -- The Law Exists (Document 1):** H.B. 288 codified vested mining use protections (Utah Code 17-81-401 to -403, formerly 17-41-501 to -503). VMU rights run with the land, cannot be zoned away, and can only be abandoned by written declaration.

**Step 2 -- Tree Farm Qualifies (Document 2):** Mining since the 1890s. Large mine permit since 1996. 725,000+ tons produced in 2009. Declaration recorded November 12, 2021.

**Step 3 -- Tree Farm Actively Exercised Its Rights (Documents 3 & 8):** NOI filed November 12, 2021. $186,000 bond. Granite Construction as operator. Pre-existing land use: "Mineral Mining."

**Step 4 -- Tree Farm Asserted Vested Rights Before the Ban (Document 5):** On February 18, 2022, Tree Farm's attorney stated on the record: "a conditional use permit from Salt Lake County will not be required on this site because Tree Farm holds a vested mining use."

**Step 5 -- The State Didn't Contest It (Document 6):** DOGM "takes no position" on vested mining use -- it's outside their jurisdiction and they won't fight it.

**Step 6 -- The County Banned Mining Anyway (Document 4):** On April 5, 2022, the County banned mining in the FR zone -- explicitly because of Tree Farm's NOI filings.

**Step 7 -- The Central Question (Document 7):** The Division said Tree Farm needs a conditional use permit. Tree Farm says vested rights eliminate that requirement. That is the question for the court.
