This is in regard to the special session back in December that was the first step to block a limestone quarry planned for
Parleys Canyon. This started the process to amend the county’s zoning ordinances to prohibit new quarries from being
developed in the Wasatch foothills. There is concern that this may conflict with a law passed in 2019 prohibiting counties
from restricting the extraction of aggregate and other “critical infrastructure materials.”
A company called Tree Farm wants to use drills and heavy explosives in Parleys Canyon near Grandeur Peak to pull out 2
million tons of crushed limestone each year. Tree Farm is a Utah company registered to developer Jesse Lassley, who
previously owned a home in the neighboring Mt. Aire Canyon. The plan was brought to the council’s attention in
November after the company submitted a notice of intent to the Utah Division of Oil, Gas and Mining.
Homeowners in Mt. Aire Canyon organized an opposition campaign and have a website https://saveparleys.org/
Tree Farm’s application for a small mine was denied, reasoning that the company’s true intent is to develop a large mine,
as opposed to the maximum 20-acre footprint allowed under a small-mine permit.
Tree Farm disputed that decision, and an appeal will be heard Feb. 23. Salt Lake City and Millcreek are asking that Tree
Farm should not be allowed to circumvent the state’s more robust permitting procedure for large mines.
Tree Farm has named Granite Construction as their operator and are calling the location the I-80 South Quarry. They have
a website https://www.i80southquarry.com/
