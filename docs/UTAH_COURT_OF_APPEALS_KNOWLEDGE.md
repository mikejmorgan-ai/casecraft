It distinguishes between BINDING PRECEDENT (law) and DICTA (non-binding judicial commentary).

## 1. PROPERTY RIGHTS AND TAKINGS

### A. Regulatory Takings - The "Rough Proportionality" Test

**B.A.M. Development, LLC v. Salt Lake County (BAM I)**
- **Citation:** 2004 UT App 34; affirmed 2006 UT 2, 128 P.3d 1161
- **BINDING HOLDING:** Utah adopted the federal Nollan/Dolan "rough proportionality" test for exactions. An exaction must meet two requirements:
  1. An "essential nexus" between a legitimate state interest and the land dedication requirement
  2. An individualized determination that the required dedication is related "both in nature and extent" to the impact of the proposed development
- **OUTCOME:** Salt Lake County's exaction requiring 13 additional feet of land dedication was found disproportionate and OVERTURNED
- **THIS IS LAW:** The rough proportionality test is binding for ALL exaction cases in Utah

---

### B. Threshold for Takings Claims

**Harold Selman Inc v. Box Elder County**
- **Citation:** 2009 UT App 45
- **BINDING HOLDING:** "A claimant must possess some protectable interest in property before that interest is entitled to recover under [the takings provision of the Utah Constitution]."
- **THIS IS LAW:** Property ownership is a THRESHOLD ISSUE - you cannot claim a taking of something you do not own

---

### C. Inverse Condemnation and Governmental Immunity

**Heughs Land v. Holladay City**
- **Citation:** 2005 UT App 351
- **BINDING HOLDING:**
  - Inverse condemnation claims are brought under Article I, Section 22 of the Utah Constitution
  - Government cannot claim immunity from takings claims
  - The governmental immunity statute (UGIA) contains a waiver for inverse condemnation
- **THIS IS LAW:** Constitutional takings claims SUPERSEDE governmental immunity

---

### D. Economic Value Deprivation Standard

**Bountiful City v. DeLuca**
- **Citation:** 826 P.2d 170 (Utah 1992)
- **BINDING HOLDING:** "A regulation becomes a compensable taking if the regulation deprives owner of a significant amount of economic value."
- **APPLICATION:** A county ordinance banning mining where mining is the highest and best use could constitute a regulatory taking
- **THIS IS LAW:** "Significant" economic deprivation, not merely "some" impact, triggers compensation

## 2. GOVERNMENT OVERREACH AND ULTRA VIRES ACTIONS

### A. Requirement for Written Findings

**McElhaney v. City of Moab**
- **Citation:** 2017 UT 65
- **BINDING HOLDING:**
  - Adjudicative land use decisions MUST include findings of fact sufficient to permit meaningful appellate review
  - Without explicit findings, a decision is "an amorphous target" that cannot be properly reviewed
- **OUTCOME:** City Council's denial of conditional use permit was REVERSED for lack of findings
- **THIS IS LAW:** Failure to make written findings renders a decision ARBITRARY AND CAPRICIOUS

---

### B. Ultra Vires Actions Are Void

**Northern Monticello Alliance v. San Juan County (NMA I & II)**
- **Citation:** 2022 UT App; 2023 UT App
- **BINDING HOLDINGS:**
  - Failure to make adequate written findings is a "fatal flaw" that invalidates subsequent appellate review
  - Commission reconsideration was "ultra vires and void" because neither LUDMA nor County Ordinance provided for such reconsideration
  - Government cannot "arrogate to itself" authority not granted by statute
- **OUTCOME:** County Commission's Amended Decision VACATED on due process grounds
- **THIS IS LAW:** Actions beyond statutory authority are VOID, not merely voidable

---

### C. Denial Must Address Specific Request

**Kilgore Companies v. Utah County Board of Adjustment**
- **Citation:** 2019 UT App 20
- **BINDING HOLDING:** Denial was arbitrary and capricious where findings did not address the specific incremental impact of the limited request
- **OUTCOME:** County's denial of conditional use permit REVERSED
- **THIS IS LAW:** Findings must address the ACTUAL REQUEST, not generalized concerns

---

### D. Public Clamor Insufficient

**Staker v. Town of Springdale**
- **Citation:** 2020 UT App 174
- **BINDING HOLDING:**
  - Public clamor ALONE cannot justify denial of land use application
  - However, public input MAY be considered as PART OF substantial evidence analysis
- **THIS IS LAW:** Neighborhood opposition alone = arbitrary and capricious

---

### E. Dillon's Rule - Limits on Municipal Power

**Provo City v. Ivie**
- **Citation:** 2004 UT 30
- **BINDING HOLDING:** Cities may ONLY exercise authority granted to them by the Legislature
- **THIS IS LAW:** If state law preempts local regulation, counties have NO authority to enact conflicting ordinances

---

## 3. STATE PREEMPTION OF LOCAL AUTHORITY

### A. Foundational Preemption Framework

**State v. Hutchinson (1980)**
- **Citation:** 624 P.2d 1116 (Utah 1980)
- **BINDING HOLDINGS:**
  1. Utah REJECTED Dillon's Rule as "archaic, unrealistic, and unresponsive"
  2. Preemption occurs when local ordinance and statute "relate to a matter that is fully, exclusively and validly covered by the statute"
  3. Courts may "look to the whole purpose and scope of the legislative scheme" to determine preemption
- **THIS IS LAW:** Two-part preemption test - direct conflict OR field occupation

---

### B. Price Development Standard

**Price Development Co. v. Orem City**
- **Citation:** 2000 UT 26, 995 P.2d 1237
- **BINDING HOLDINGS:**
  1. "An ordinance is invalid if it intrudes into an area which the Legislature has preempted by comprehensive legislation intended to blanket a particular field"
  2. "Local governments may legislate... provided the ordinance in no way conflicts with existing state law"
  3. Courts examine statute's "structure and purpose" to determine preemptive intent
- **THIS IS LAW:** Analytical framework for all future preemption analysis

---

### C. Strict Construction of Restrictive Zoning

**Patterson v. Utah County Board of Adjustment**
- **Citation:** 893 P.2d 602 (Utah Ct. App. 1995)
- **BINDING HOLDINGS:**
  1. "Provisions restricting property uses should be STRICTLY CONSTRUED"
  2. "Provisions permitting property uses should be LIBERALLY CONSTRUED in favor of the property owner"
  3. Interpretation of zoning ordinances by a board is NOT entitled to deference
- **THIS IS LAW:** Courts interpret zoning ordinances de novo, favoring property owners

---

### D. State Permits vs. Local Restrictions

**Critical Infrastructure Materials Act Framework**
- **Statutory Law (Utah Code 17-41-502):**
  - "Notwithstanding a political subdivision's prohibition, restriction, or other limitation... the rights of a mine operator with a vested mining use include the rights to progress, extend, enlarge, grow, or expand the vested mining use"
  - Mine operator "PRESUMED to have a right to expand the vested mining use to new land"
- **THIS IS LAW:** State-permitted operations cannot be prohibited by local ordinance

## 4. VESTED RIGHTS DOCTRINE

### A. Foundational Case

**Western Land Equities, Inc. v. City of Logan**
- **Citation:** 617 P.2d 388 (Utah 1980)
- **BINDING HOLDING:** "A property owner should be able to plan for developing his property in a manner permitted by existing zoning regulations with some degree of assurance that the basic ground rules will not be changed in mid-stream"
- **THIS IS LAW:** Foundation of vested rights in Utah - cited in virtually all subsequent cases

---

### B. Statutory Vested Mining Rights (Utah Code Section 17-41-501)

**BINDING STATUTORY PROVISIONS:**

| Provision | Text | Legal Effect |
|-----------|------|--------------|
| §17-41-501(1)(a) | Mining use "CONCLUSIVELY PRESUMED" to be vested if existed before restriction | Strongest possible presumption |
| §17-41-501(1)(b) | Challenger has burden to prove by "CLEAR AND CONVINCING EVIDENCE" | Higher than preponderance |
| §17-41-501(2)(a) | Vested mining use "RUNS WITH THE LAND" | Transfers automatically with ownership |
| §17-41-501(3) | Permit boundaries do NOT limit scope of rights | Expansion permitted |

---

### C. Nonconforming Uses as Vested Rights

**Legal Principle (BINDING):**
- A valid legal nonconforming use is a vested property right in Utah
- Mandatory amortization deprives property owner of a vested right
- If government desires to interfere with lawful use that is not a nuisance nor abandoned, it MUST compensate the owner

---

### D. Doctrine of Diminishing Assets

**Gibbons & Reed Co. v. North Salt Lake City**
- **Citation:** 431 P.2d 559 (Utah 1967)
- **BINDING HOLDING:** Extractive businesses can expand beyond original physical boundaries because "the very nature and use of an extractive business contemplates the continuance of such use of the entire parcel of land"
- **THIS IS LAW:** Utah's "solitary exception" to the general prohibition on expansion of nonconforming uses - applies to mining operations

## 5. DUE PROCESS IN LAND USE

### A. Constitutional Standards

**Utah Constitution Article I, Section 7:**
"No person shall be deprived of life, liberty or property, without due process of law."

### B. Property Interest Requirement

**Heideman v. Washington City**
- **Citation:** 2007 UT App 11, 155 P.3d 900
- **BINDING HOLDING:** Plaintiff must have more than "an abstract need or unilateral expectation" - must have a "legitimate claim of entitlement"

---

### C. Notice and Hearing Requirements

**Salt Lake City Corp. v. Jordan River Restoration Network**
- **Citation:** 2012 UT 84, 299 P.3d 990
- **BINDING HOLDING:** Due process requires, at minimum, "adequate notice and an opportunity to be heard in a meaningful manner" including opportunity to present "evidence, objections, and arguments"
- **THIS IS LAW:** Minimum due process standard for all property proceedings

---

### D. Due Process Violations Void Statutes of Limitations

**Jordan v. Jensen**
- **Citation:** 2017 UT 1
- **BINDING HOLDING:** "Statutes of limitations will not apply when triggered by constitutionally defective state action"
- **THIS IS LAW:** Constitutional violations cannot be cured by time limits

## 6. STANDARDS OF REVIEW

| Decision Type | Standard | Source |
|--------------|----------|--------|
| **Land Use Regulation (Legislative)** | "Reasonably debatable" | Utah Code §10-9a-801(3)(a) |
| **Land Use Decision (Administrative)** | Arbitrary, capricious, or illegal | Utah Code §17-27a-801(3) |
| **Illegality Determination** | Correctness (some deference) | Fox v. Park City, 2008 UT 85 |
| **Substantial Evidence** | Whether reasonable minds could reach same conclusion | Utah Code §17-27a-801(3)(c) |
| **Due Process Violations** | De novo | Constitutional standard |
| **Ordinance Interpretation** | De novo (no deference) | Patterson v. Utah County |

## 7. KEY STATUTORY PROVISIONS

### Utah Code §17-41-402 (Now §17-81-302) - EXPRESS PREEMPTION

```
(5) A POLITICAL SUBDIVISION MAY NOT change the zoning designation of
or a zoning regulation affecting land within a MINING PROTECTION AREA
unless the political subdivision receives WRITTEN APPROVAL for the
change from EACH MINE OPERATOR within the area.

(6) A political subdivision may not adopt, enact, or amend an existing
land use regulation, ordinance, or regulation that would PROHIBIT,
RESTRICT, REGULATE, or otherwise LIMIT critical infrastructure
materials operations, including vested critical infrastructure
materials operations.
```

**LEGAL EFFECT:**
- "May not" = MANDATORY PROHIBITION - counties have NO discretion
- This is EXPRESS PREEMPTION - the strongest form
- ANY local ordinance violating this section is VOID

---

### Utah Constitution Article I, Section 22 - Takings/Damagings Clause

```
"Private property shall not be taken or damaged for public use
without just compensation."
```

**THIS IS LAW:** Utah protects against both TAKINGS and DAMAGINGS - BROADER than federal Fifth Amendment

## SUMMARY: BINDING LAW vs. DICTA

### What Constitutes BINDING PRECEDENT:
1. **Holdings** - The court's direct answer to the legal question
2. **Ratios decidendi** - Legal reasoning necessary to reach the holding
3. **Statutory interpretations** - How courts construe statutes
4. **Tests established** - Multi-factor tests that must be applied

### What Constitutes DICTA (Non-Binding):
1. **Obiter dicta** - Comments not necessary to the decision
2. **Hypotheticals** - "If the facts were different..."
3. **General observations** - Background discussion
4. **Policy commentary** - What the law should be (vs. what it is)
5. **Discussion of other jurisdictions** - Comparative analysis

---

## APPLICATION TO COUNTY MINING BAN CASES

**STRONGEST ARGUMENTS BASED ON BINDING PRECEDENT:**

1. **Express Preemption (§17-41-402):** Counties "may not" prohibit or restrict permitted mining operations - mandatory prohibition

2. **Vested Rights (§17-41-501):** Mining use is "conclusively presumed" vested if it pre-dates restriction; burden on county to disprove by clear and convincing evidence

3. **Regulatory Taking (Bountiful City v. DeLuca):** If ban deprives owner of "significant amount of economic value," it may constitute compensable taking

4. **Due Process (McElhaney):** If county failed to make detailed findings, decision is arbitrary and capricious

5. **Diminishing Assets (Gibbons & Reed):** For extractive uses, expansion is permitted - restriction effectively terminates the use
