## 1. PROPERTY RIGHTS AND TAKINGS

### A. Utah's Takings Clause - BROADER Than Federal

**Utah Constitution Article I, Section 22:**
> "Private property shall not be taken **or damaged** for public use without just compensation."

**BINDING LAW:** Utah's constitution protects against both TAKINGS and DAMAGINGS - this is BROADER than the federal Fifth Amendment which only addresses takings.

**Colman v. Utah State Land Board**
- **Citation:** 795 P.2d 622 (Utah 1990)
- **BINDING HOLDING:** Article I, Section 22 is SELF-EXECUTING. Inverse condemnation claims can be brought directly under the constitution without enabling legislation.
- **THIS IS LAW:** The State is NOT immune for takings claims

---

### B. Regulatory Takings Standard

**Bountiful City v. DeLuca**
- **Citation:** 826 P.2d 170 (Utah 1992)
- **BINDING HOLDING:** "Regulation of property for the public good becomes a compensable taking if the regulation deprives the owner of a **significant amount** of economic value."
- **THIS IS LAW:** Utah's articulation of the regulatory takings threshold

---

### C. Penn Central Analysis (Applied in Utah)

**THE THREE-FACTOR TEST (BINDING FEDERAL LAW):**
1. **Economic impact** of the regulation on the claimant
2. **Investment-backed expectations** - extent to which regulation interferes with distinct, reasonable expectations
3. **Character of government action** - physical invasion vs. regulatory interference

**Utah Application:**
- Courts examine the "parcel as a whole" - do not segment single parcel into discrete interests
- Investment-backed expectations measured at TIME OF ACQUISITION
- BUT: Property acquired with knowledge of historical mining use carries different expectations

---

### D. Lucas Per Se Takings

**Lucas v. South Carolina Coastal Council**
- **Citation:** 505 U.S. 1003 (1992) - Federal, binding in Utah
- **BINDING HOLDING:** Regulation that deprives owner of ALL economically viable use is a PER SE TAKING requiring compensation
- **Exception:** Unless restriction inheres in title itself (background property/nuisance law)

**APPLICATION TO MINERAL RIGHTS:**
- If mineral rights are legally SEVERED from surface rights (separate ownership), they may be treated as distinct parcels
- Complete elimination of mineral estate value while surface value remains may trigger Lucas analysis on mineral estate alone

---

### E. Exactions - Rough Proportionality

**B.A.M. Development, LLC v. Salt Lake County (BAM I, II, III)**
- **Citation:** 2006 UT 2, 128 P.3d 1161
- **BINDING HOLDING:** The Nollan/Dolan "rough proportionality" test applies to ALL development exactions in Utah

**TWO-PART TEST (BINDING):**
1. **Essential nexus** between legitimate state interest and land dedication requirement
2. **Individualized determination** that dedication is related both in nature AND extent to proposed development's impact

**Sheetz v. County of El Dorado (2024)**
- **Citation:** 601 U.S. ___ (2024) - Federal, binding in Utah
- **BINDING HOLDING:** Nollan/Dolan applies to ALL exactions, including legislatively imposed permit conditions (consistent with Utah's B.A.M. approach)

---

### F. Access Rights Are Compensable

**UDOT v. Harvey Real Estate**
- **Citation:** 2017 UT 36
- **BINDING HOLDING:** "The rights of access, light, and air are easements appurtenant to the land of an abutting owner" and constitute COMPENSABLE property interests

## 2. VESTED RIGHTS DOCTRINE

### A. The Foundational Case

**Western Land Equities, Inc. v. City of Logan**
- **Citation:** 617 P.2d 388 (Utah 1980)
- **BINDING HOLDING:** "An applicant for subdivision approval or a building permit is ENTITLED to favorable action if the application conforms to the zoning ordinance in effect at the time of the application"

**EXCEPTIONS (BINDING):**
1. Changes in zoning ordinances are PENDING which would prohibit the use applied for, OR
2. Municipality can show a COMPELLING REASON for exercising police power retroactively

**KEY PRINCIPLES:**
- "An applicant should not be subject to shifting policies that do not reflect serious public concerns"
- Local government must "act in good faith and not reject an application to simply substitute the judgment of current officials for that of their predecessors"

**WHAT WAS UPHELD:** City was ESTOPPED from enforcing zoning change against plaintiffs' conforming application

---

### B. Vested Rights Based on Estoppel, Not Constitutional Rights

**Patterson v. American Fork City**
- **Citation:** 2003 UT 7, 67 P.3d 466
- **BINDING HOLDING:** "The Vested Rights Rule is NOT based on constitutional or property rights, but **estoppel** - that is, detrimental reliance on a local zoning ordinance"
- **THIS IS LAW:** Vested rights require reasonable RELIANCE

---

### C. Nonconforming Uses as Vested Rights

**Swenson v. Salt Lake City**
- **Citation:** 16 Utah 2d 111, 396 P.2d 625 (1965)
- **BINDING HOLDING:** "A nonconforming use in Utah is a type of **vested property right**."
- **THIS IS LAW:** "The general rule is against retrospective operation of an ordinance, where vested rights are concerned"

---

### D. Statutory Vested Mining Rights

**Utah Code Section 17-41-501 (Now Section 17-81-401) - BINDING STATUTORY LAW:**

| Provision | Text | Legal Effect |
|-----------|------|--------------|
| §17-41-501(1)(a) | Mining use "**CONCLUSIVELY PRESUMED**" to be vested if existed before restriction | Cannot be rebutted - strongest presumption |
| §17-41-501(1)(b) | Challenger has burden to prove by "**CLEAR AND CONVINCING EVIDENCE**" | Higher than preponderance of evidence |
| §17-41-501(2)(a) | Vested mining use "**RUNS WITH THE LAND**" | Transfers automatically with property ownership |
| §17-41-501(2)(b) | May be "**CHANGED TO ANOTHER MINING USE**" without losing vested status | Flexibility in operations |
| §17-41-501(3) | Permit boundaries "**DO NOT LIMIT**" scope of mine operator's rights | Expansion beyond permit boundaries permitted |

**CRITICAL INTERPRETATION:**
The "on or before January 1, 2019" language in §17-41-101(13) defining "mine operator" is a ONE-TIME THRESHOLD, not an ongoing requirement. Once commercial quantities were produced before that date, the status is PERMANENT.

---

### E. Vested Rights in Actions

**Halling v. Industrial Comm'n of Utah (1927)**
- **BINDING HOLDING:** "A vested right of action is property in the same sense in which tangible things are property, and is equally protected against arbitrary interference"

## 3. MINERAL RIGHTS AND SEVERED ESTATES

### A. Constitutional Protection of Mineral Rights

**Jordan v. Jensen**
- **Citation:** 2017 UT 1, 391 P.3d 183
- **BINDING HOLDINGS:**
  1. Severed mineral rights are **DISTINCT PROPERTY INTERESTS** protected by due process
  2. Tax sale conducted without constitutionally adequate notice to mineral owners does NOT transfer those interests
  3. Statutes of limitations "will NOT apply when triggered by constitutionally defective state action"
  4. Failure to provide notice is a **JURISDICTIONAL DEFECT**

**WHAT WAS OVERTURNED:** Hansen v. Morris (1955) - expressly overruled regarding limitations periods and due process violations

**THIS IS LAW:** Mineral rights owners have VESTED PROPERTY RIGHTS that cannot be defeated without due process

---

### B. Doctrine of Diminishing Assets

**Gibbons & Reed Co. v. North Salt Lake City**
- **Citation:** 431 P.2d 559 (Utah 1967)
- **BINDING HOLDING:** For extractive businesses (mining, gravel), legal nonconforming uses can EXPAND BEYOND original physical boundaries because "the very nature and use of an extractive business contemplates the continuance of such use of the entire parcel of land"

**REASONING:**
- "It constitutes a diminishing asset and is consumed in the very process of use"
- "Ordinary concept of 'use' must yield to the realities of the business"

**WHAT WAS UPHELD:** Injunction prohibiting enforcement of zoning that would require discontinuance of gravel operations

**THIS IS LAW:** Utah's "solitary exception" to prohibition on expansion of nonconforming uses - HAS NOT BEEN OVERTURNED

---

## 4. STATE PREEMPTION FRAMEWORK

### A. Foundational Case: Rejection of Dillon's Rule

**State v. Hutchinson**
- **Citation:** 624 P.2d 1116 (Utah 1980)
- **BINDING HOLDINGS:**
  1. Utah REJECTED Dillon's Rule as "archaic, unrealistic, and unresponsive to the current needs of both state and local governments"
  2. Preemption occurs when ordinance and statute "relate to a matter that is fully, exclusively and validly covered by the statute"
  3. Courts may "look to the whole purpose and scope of the legislative scheme" to determine preemption
  4. Provisions must be "contradictory in the sense that they cannot coexist" for conflict

**THIS IS LAW:** Two-part preemption test - DIRECT CONFLICT or FIELD OCCUPATION

---

### B. Preemption Analytical Framework

**Price Development Co. v. Orem City**
- **Citation:** 2000 UT 26, 995 P.2d 1237
- **BINDING HOLDINGS:**
  1. "An ordinance is invalid if it intrudes into an area which the Legislature has preempted by comprehensive legislation intended to blanket a particular field"
  2. Courts examine statute's "structure and purpose" to determine preemptive intent
  3. If objectives of local ordinance and state law are "clearly different," no preemption

**THIS IS LAW:** Framework for analyzing all preemption claims in Utah

---

### C. Limits on Municipal Power

**Provo City v. Ivie**
- **Citation:** 2004 UT 30, 94 P.3d 206
- **BINDING HOLDINGS:**
  1. Non-chartered municipalities derive authority EXCLUSIVELY from legislative authorization
  2. Cities have only powers EXPRESSLY GRANTED and those NECESSARILY IMPLIED
  3. Courts will not interfere unless action is arbitrary, **directly prohibited by state law**, or **inconsistent with state policy**

**THIS IS LAW:** When state law says counties "may not" do something, counties LACK AUTHORITY to act

---

### D. Express Preemption Statute - Mining

**Utah Code §17-41-402 (Now §17-81-302) - BINDING STATUTORY LAW:**

```
(5) A POLITICAL SUBDIVISION MAY NOT change the zoning designation of
or a zoning regulation affecting land within a MINING PROTECTION AREA
unless the political subdivision receives WRITTEN APPROVAL for the
change from EACH MINE OPERATOR within the area.

(6) A political subdivision may not adopt, enact, or amend an existing
land use regulation, ordinance, or regulation that would PROHIBIT,
RESTRICT, REGULATE, or otherwise LIMIT critical infrastructure
materials operations, including vested critical infrastructure
materials operations.
```

**LEGAL EFFECT:**
- "May not" = MANDATORY PROHIBITION - counties have ZERO discretion
- This is EXPRESS PREEMPTION - the strongest form
- Any local ordinance violating this section is VOID

---

### E. Mandatory Statutory Language

**BINDING INTERPRETATION:**
- "SHALL" / "MUST" = Mandatory duty
- "MAY NOT" / "SHALL NOT" = Absolute prohibition
- "MAY" = Permissive only

When statute says "may not prohibit or restrict," local government has NO AUTHORITY to prohibit or restrict.

## 5. GOVERNMENT OVERREACH AND LIMITS ON POWER

### A. Arbitrary and Capricious Standard

**Springville Citizens for a Better Community v. City of Springville**
- **Citation:** 1999 UT 25, 979 P.2d 332
- **BINDING HOLDINGS:**
  1. Land use decision is arbitrary and capricious if "not supported by substantial evidence"
  2. Decision is ILLEGAL if city violated its own ordinances during approval
  3. Words "shall" and "must" in ordinances are ALWAYS mandatory
  4. Cities are "bound by the terms and standards of applicable zoning ordinances and are not at liberty to make land use decisions in derogation thereof"

**THIS IS LAW:** Cities CANNOT change rules mid-process; illegal decisions not protected by presumption of validity

---

### B. Revised Prejudice Standard

**Potter v. South Salt Lake City**
- **Citation:** 2018 UT 22, 422 P.3d 803
- **BINDING HOLDING:** Party challenging error NO LONGER required to show "decision would have been different" - only need show "reasonable likelihood that the error changed the decision"
- **THIS IS LAW:** LOWERED BAR for challenging government land use errors (overruling stricter Springville standard)

---

### C. Standards of Review

**Fox v. Park City**
- **Citation:** 2008 UT 85, 200 P.3d 182
- **BINDING HOLDINGS:**
  1. Review limited to whether decision is "arbitrary, capricious, or illegal"
  2. Arbitrary/capricious = not supported by substantial evidence
  3. Illegal = violates law, statute, or ordinance in effect at time
  4. Appellate court reviews land use authority DIRECTLY, affording NO deference to district court

**SUBSTANTIAL EVIDENCE DEFINED:** "That quantum and quality of relevant evidence that is adequate to convince a reasonable mind to support a conclusion"

---

### D. Equal Protection - Class of One

**Patterson v. American Fork City**
- **Citation:** 2003 UT 7, 67 P.3d 466
- **BINDING HOLDINGS:**
  1. "Absent invidious discrimination, the conventional planning dispute is a matter primarily of concern to the state and does not implicate the Constitution"
  2. "Uneven enforcement is NOT sufficient - requires showing of **totally illegitimate animus**"

**Village of Willowbrook v. Olech**
- **Citation:** 528 U.S. 562 (2000) - Federal, binding in Utah
- **BINDING HOLDING:** "Class of one" equal protection claims recognized where plaintiff shows:
  1. Intentionally treated differently from similarly situated persons
  2. No rational basis for different treatment
  3. Municipality was arbitrary, intentional, and/or spiteful

---

### E. Ultra Vires Actions

**Chambers v. Smithfield City**
- **Citation:** 714 P.2d 1133 (Utah 1986)
- **BINDING HOLDING:** Local ordinances that conflict with state enabling statutes are VOID and unenforceable
- **THIS IS LAW:** Municipalities cannot alter statutory schemes

**Scherbel v. Salt Lake City Corp.**
- **Citation:** 758 P.2d 897 (Utah 1988)
- **BINDING HOLDINGS:**
  1. SEPARATION OF POWERS: "Authority to resolve zoning disputes is properly an executive function rather than a legislative one"
  2. City council may NOT hear appeals from planning commission decisions under council-mayor form

---

### F. Police Power Limits

**Gibbons & Reed Company v. North Salt Lake City**
- **Citation:** 431 P.2d 559 (Utah 1967)
- **BINDING HOLDINGS:**
  1. Power to zone "is derived from the state"
  2. Right to regulate property "limited to purposes of promoting health, safety, morals, and general welfare"
  3. "A zoning ordinance is not unconstitutional unless it is **clearly arbitrary and unreasonable, having no substantial relation** to public health, safety, morals, or general welfare"

## 6. DUE PROCESS REQUIREMENTS

### A. Property Interest Required

**Petersen v. Riverton City**
- **Citation:** 2010 UT 58, 243 P.3d 1261
- **BINDING HOLDING:** To prevail on due process claim, must establish defendant's actions "deprived plaintiff of a protectable property interest"

**Property Interest Defined:**
- "A legitimate claim of entitlement" to some benefit
- NOT merely "an abstract need or unilateral expectation"
- Exists only where "existing rules and understandings... secure certain benefits and support claims of entitlement"

---

### B. Notice and Hearing

**Salt Lake City Corp. v. Jordan River Restoration Network**
- **Citation:** 2012 UT 84, 299 P.3d 990
- **BINDING HOLDING:** Due process requires, at minimum, "adequate notice and an opportunity to be heard in a meaningful manner" including opportunity to present "evidence, objections, and arguments"

---

### C. Constitutional Violations Exception to Limitations

**Jordan v. Jensen**
- **Citation:** 2017 UT 1
- **BINDING HOLDING:** "Statutes of limitations will NOT apply when triggered by constitutionally defective state action"
- **THIS IS LAW:** Constitutional due process violations cannot be cured by passage of time

---

### D. Written Findings Required

**McElhaney v. City of Moab**
- **Citation:** 2017 UT 65
- **BINDING HOLDING:** Adjudicative land use decisions MUST include findings of fact sufficient to permit meaningful appellate review
- **THIS IS LAW:** Failure to make findings = fatal flaw = arbitrary and capricious

## DISTINGUISHING BINDING HOLDINGS FROM DICTA

### BINDING PRECEDENT (This is LAW):
1. **Holdings** - Court's direct answer to the legal question
2. **Legal tests established** - Multi-factor tests that MUST be applied
3. **Statutory interpretations** - How courts construe statutes controls future cases
4. **Constitutional interpretations** - Binding on all lower courts

### DICTA (Persuasive but NOT Binding):
1. **Obiter dicta** - Comments not necessary to the decision
2. **Hypotheticals** - "If the facts were different..."
3. **Policy discussions** - What the law should be vs. what it is
4. **Other jurisdictions** - Comparative analysis
5. **Concurring/dissenting opinions** - Not the court's holding

## SYNTHESIS FOR MINING RIGHTS LITIGATION

### Strongest Arguments for Property Owner (Based on BINDING Precedent):

1. **Express Preemption (§17-41-402):** State law says counties "may not prohibit or restrict" - ordinance banning mining is VOID

2. **Vested Rights (§17-41-501):** Mining use "conclusively presumed" vested; burden on county to disprove by clear and convincing evidence; rights "run with the land"

3. **Diminishing Assets (Gibbons & Reed):** Extractive uses have special expansion rights - restriction effectively terminates the use

4. **Mineral Rights Protection (Jordan v. Jensen):** Mineral rights are distinct property interests requiring due process

5. **Constitutional Takings (Article I, §22):** Complete ban may constitute per se taking or at minimum trigger Penn Central analysis

### Potential County Defenses (and Weaknesses):

| Defense | Weakness |
|---------|----------|
| Valid police power | Preemption statute EXPRESSLY limits authority |
| No continuous operations | Statute uses "on or before" - one-time threshold, not continuous requirement |
| Property retains other value | Severed mineral estate may be analyzed separately under Lucas |
| General environmental protection | Cannot trump express state preemption |
