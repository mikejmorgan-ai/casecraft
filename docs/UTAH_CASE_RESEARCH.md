# Utah Legal Cases for AI Prediction System Training

Below is a curated collection of Utah cases across four categories suitable for training the CaseCraft AI prediction system. These cases feature clear rulings, well-documented legal reasoning, and precedent-setting value ideal for blind prediction testing.

---

## 1. PROPERTY RIGHTS CASES

### Utah Stream Access Coalition v. VR Acquisitions, LLC (USAC II)
- **Citation:** 2019 UT 62
- **Court:** Utah Supreme Court
- **Date:** October 2019
- **Summary:** Long-running dispute over whether Utahns enjoy a public easement right to access private lands to use public waters for recreational purposes.
- **Outcome:** Private property owners won. The Court unanimously held that historical evidence "did not establish any 19th-century basis for the existence of such an easement."
- **Training Value:** Critical for understanding public easement doctrine, historical easement analysis, and balancing public access against private property rights.
- **Source:** [Utah Supreme Court Analysis](https://fedsoc.org/scdw/utah-supreme-court-grapples-with-public-recreational-access-to-public-waters-via-private-property)

---

### Bell Canyon Acres Homeowners Ass'n v. McLelland
- **Citation:** 2019 UT 54
- **Court:** Utah Supreme Court
- **Date:** 2019
- **Summary:** Neighbors objected to fences encroaching on bridle path easements. Issue was whether plaintiffs needed to sue all homeowners whose property was subject to the easement.
- **Outcome:** Plaintiffs won on procedural issue. Court reversed lower court, holding that objecting neighbors only need to sue those with infringing fences, not all easement-affected homeowners.
- **Training Value:** Clarifies standing requirements and party joinder in easement disputes.
- **Source:** [Property Rights Case Summaries](https://propertyrights.utah.gov/case-summaries/)

---

### UDOT v. Harvey Real Estate
- **Citation:** 2017 UT 36
- **Court:** Utah Supreme Court
- **Date:** 2017
- **Summary:** Condemnation case addressing whether access rights constitute compensable property interests.
- **Outcome:** Property owner prevailed. Court recognized that "the rights of access, light, and air are easements appurtenant to the land of an abutting owner on a street" and constitute property rights requiring just compensation.
- **Training Value:** Essential precedent for access rights, easement valuation, and condemnation law.
- **Source:** [UDOT v. Harvey Real Estate](https://propertyrights.utah.gov/udot-v-harvey-real-estate)

---

### Nyman v. Anchor Development, LLC
- **Citation:** 2003 UT 27
- **Court:** Utah Supreme Court
- **Date:** 2003
- **Summary:** Dispute over balancing interests of property owners subject to easements.
- **Outcome:** Established key principle: "Whenever there is ownership of property subject to an easement there is a dichotomy of interests, both of which must be respected and kept in balance."
- **Training Value:** Foundational case for easement interpretation and balancing of rights doctrine.
- **Source:** [Cases Listed Alphabetically](https://propertyrights.utah.gov/find-the-law/appellate-decisions/cases-listed-alphabetically/)

---

### 2025 UT App 54 - Eminent Domain for Private Development
- **Citation:** 2025 UT App 54
- **Court:** Utah Court of Appeals
- **Date:** 2025
- **Summary:** Private landowner sought to use eminent domain to condemn easement rights for private development.
- **Outcome:** Landowner lost. Court held that a private landowner has no authority to exercise eminent domain to condemn easement rights for private development.
- **Training Value:** Clear ruling on limits of eminent domain authority for private parties.
- **Source:** [Case Summaries](https://propertyrights.utah.gov/case-summaries/)

## 2. VESTED MINING RIGHTS CASES

### Gibbons & Reed Co. v. North Salt Lake City
- **Citation:** 431 P.2d 559 (Utah 1967)
- **Court:** Utah Supreme Court
- **Date:** 1967
- **Summary:** Established the "doctrine of diminishing assets" for nonconforming gravel pit operations.
- **Outcome:** Gravel company prevailed. The doctrine permits legal nonconforming gravel pits to expand beyond their historical physical boundaries to continue operations.
- **Training Value:** Seminal case for vested rights in extractive industries; exception to normal nonconforming use restrictions.
- **Source:** [Vested Rights - Property Rights Ombudsman](https://propertyrights.utah.gov/find-the-law/legal-topics/vested-rights/)

---

### Draper City v. Geneva Rock Products (Point of the Mountain)
- **Citation:** Third District Court, Salt Lake County (2022-ongoing)
- **Court:** Utah District Court
- **Date:** 2022
- **Summary:** City challenged Geneva Rock's claim that its quarry constitutes a "vested mining use" exempt from local zoning review. State regulators recognized vested rights for 67-acre expansion within city limits.
- **Outcome:** Pending/ongoing litigation.
- **Training Value:** Tests intersection of Critical Infrastructure Materials Operations legislation (HB288), vested rights doctrine, and municipal zoning authority.
- **Source:** [Salt Lake Tribune Coverage](https://www.sltrib.com/news/environment/2022/09/27/draper-takes-geneva-rock-court/)

---

### Kilgore Companies v. Stockton Town/Tooele County
- **Citation:** Utah Property Rights Ombudsman Advisory Opinion 217
- **Court:** Administrative/Advisory
- **Date:** 2019
- **Summary:** Kilgore claimed mining rights on property zoned agricultural, arguing legal nonconforming use, a settlement agreement, and Critical Infrastructure Materials legislation protected operations.
- **Outcome:** Town prevailed. Kilgore had not established that Critical Infrastructure Materials legislation protected mining on the property; Stockton has authority to prohibit mining.
- **Training Value:** Tests limits of state preemption, nonconforming use doctrine, and vested rights claims.
- **Source:** [Advisory Opinion 217](https://propertyrights.utah.gov/advisory-opinions-151-300/advisory-opinion-217/)

---

### Jordan v. Jensen
- **Citation:** 2017 UT 1
- **Court:** Utah Supreme Court
- **Date:** 2017
- **Summary:** Mineral rights owners challenged tax sale of surface property where they never received notice. County sold property at tax sale without notifying severed mineral interest owners.
- **Outcome:** Mineral owners prevailed. Court invalidated tax title as to severed minerals on due process grounds, holding that statutes of limitations "will not apply when triggered by constitutionally defective state action."
- **Training Value:** Critical for mineral rights severance, due process in property proceedings, and vested property rights.
- **Source:** [Utah Supreme Court Invalidates Tax Title](https://www.theoilandgasreport.com/2017/03/16/utah-supreme-court-invalidates-tax-title-as-to-severed-minerals-on-due-process-grounds/)

---

### United Park City Mines Co. v. Estate of Clegg
- **Citation:** 737 P.2d 173 (Utah 1987)
- **Court:** Utah Supreme Court
- **Date:** 1987
- **Summary:** Dispute over mineral rights ownership and mining claims.
- **Outcome:** Established principles for mineral estate rights in Utah.
- **Training Value:** Foundation case for understanding mineral rights separation and mining claim validity.
- **Source:** [Justia - United Park City Mines](https://law.justia.com/cases/utah/supreme-court/1987/737-p-2d-173.html)

---

### Snake Creek Mining & Tunnel Co. v. Midway Irrigation Co.
- **Citation:** 260 U.S. 596 (1923)
- **Court:** U.S. Supreme Court
- **Date:** 1923
- **Summary:** Utah mining company claimed vested rights to underground waters under common law rule when tunnel was constructed. Challenged subsequent changes to Utah water law.
- **Outcome:** Established that rights vested under existing law are not affected by subsequent legal changes.
- **Training Value:** Historic precedent on vested rights doctrine and retroactivity of law changes.
- **Source:** [Justia - Snake Creek Mining](https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/260/596/)

## 3. FUGITIVE DUST / AIR QUALITY CASES

### Ralph Smith Co. v. Air Quality Board
- **Citation:** 2000 UT App 212
- **Court:** Utah Court of Appeals
- **Date:** 2000
- **Summary:** Company challenged citation for fugitive dust violation, arguing single inadequate opacity reading was insufficient basis.
- **Outcome:** Air Quality Board prevailed. Court affirmed that liability for failing to minimize fugitive dust under R307-12-3(3.B)(1) is not limited to site owners/operators and is not dependent solely on opacity standards.
- **Training Value:** Clarifies liability standards for fugitive dust violations; distinction between opacity standards and minimization requirements.
- **Source:** [Ralph Smith Co. v. Air Quality Bd.](https://law.justia.com/cases/utah/court-of-appeals-unpublished/2000/ralphsmith.html)

---

### Utah Physicians for a Healthy Environment v. Kennecott (Rio Tinto)
- **Citation:** U.S. District Court, District of Utah (2011-2014)
- **Court:** Federal District Court
- **Date:** 2013-2014
- **Summary:** Coalition of doctors, mothers, and environmental groups sued Rio Tinto's Kennecott subsidiary over air pollution at Bingham Canyon mine. Alleged failure to obtain EPA approval for increased emissions and expanded mining since 2007.
- **Outcome:** Kennecott/Rio Tinto prevailed. Court ruled in favor of the mining company.
- **Training Value:** Federal court treatment of Clean Air Act citizen suits; mining operations and air quality compliance.
- **Source:** [Mining.com - Kennecott Lawsuit](https://www.mining.com/rio-tintos-kennecott-wins-clean-air-lawsuit-in-the-us/)

---

### EPA Salt Lake City PM2.5 Attainment Determination
- **Citation:** 85 FR 73379 (2020)
- **Court/Agency:** EPA Administrative Action
- **Date:** November 2020
- **Summary:** EPA determined Salt Lake City and Provo areas attained PM2.5 standards by December 31, 2019 deadline. Western Resource Advocates challenged based on inadequate near-road monitoring data.
- **Outcome:** EPA determination upheld. Areas redesignated to attainment status.
- **Training Value:** Illustrates regulatory challenge process, monitoring requirements, and Clean Air Act attainment standards.
- **Source:** [Federal Register - PM2.5 Determination](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2020/11/17/2020-24443/determination-of-attainment-by-the-attainment-date-for-the-salt-lake-city-utah-and-provo-utah-2006)

## 4. CITY/COUNTY OVERREACH CASES

### B.A.M. Development, LLC v. Salt Lake County (BAM I, II, III)
- **Citation:** 2004 UT App 34; 2006 UT 2
- **Court:** Utah Court of Appeals; Utah Supreme Court
- **Date:** 2004, 2006
- **Summary:** County required developer to dedicate 13 additional feet of land abutting 3500 South before approving subdivision plat. Developer challenged under Nollan/Dolan framework.
- **Outcome:** Developer prevailed. Court applied "rough proportionality" test, establishing that exactions must be proportional to development impacts.
- **Training Value:** Utah's leading exaction case; application of federal Nollan/Dolan standards to local government conditions.
- **Source:** [B.A.M. v. Salt Lake County](https://law.justia.com/cases/utah/court-of-appeals-published/2004/bam022004.html)

---

### McElhaney v. City of Moab
- **Citation:** 2017 UT 65
- **Court:** Utah Supreme Court
- **Date:** 2017
- **Summary:** City Council denied bed and breakfast conditional use permit without making explicit factual findings. Owners appealed.
- **Outcome:** Property owners prevailed on procedural grounds. Court held that adjudicative land use decisions must include findings of fact sufficient to permit meaningful appellate review.
- **Training Value:** Essential case on procedural requirements for land use denials; due process in administrative decisions.
- **Source:** [Snell & Wilmer Analysis](https://www.swlaw.com/blogs/environmental-and-natural-resources/2017/12/21/utah-supreme-court-clarifies-administrative-agencies-fact-finding-duty-and-the-standard-of-review-for-administrative-appeals/)

---

### Staker v. Town of Springdale
- **Citation:** 2020 UT App 174
- **Court:** Utah Court of Appeals
- **Date:** 2020
- **Summary:** Town denied conditional use permit for parking, citing potential interference with surrounding properties. Applicant alleged decision was based on "public clamor" rather than evidence.
- **Outcome:** Town prevailed. Court held that while public clamor alone cannot justify denial, the Town properly considered public input as part of substantial evidence analysis.
- **Training Value:** Distinguishes proper vs. improper use of public comment in land use decisions.
- **Source:** [Staker v. Town of Springdale](https://propertyrights.utah.gov/2020/12/31/new-case-summary-staker-v-town-of-springdale/)

---

### Northern Monticello Alliance v. San Juan County (NMA I & II)
- **Citation:** 2022 UT App (NMA I); 2023 UT App (NMA II)
- **Court:** Utah Court of Appeals
- **Date:** 2022, 2023
- **Summary:** Third party challenged revocation of conditional use permit, raising due process concerns about participation rights. NMA II addressed adequacy of written findings.
- **Outcome:** In NMA II, property owners prevailed. Court held failure to make adequate written findings was a "fatal flaw" that invalidated subsequent appellate review.
- **Training Value:** Clarifies third-party standing and procedural requirements in land use appeals.
- **Source:** [Case Summaries](https://propertyrights.utah.gov/case-summaries/)

---

### Kilgore Companies v. Utah County Board of Adjustment
- **Citation:** 2019 UT App 20
- **Court:** Utah Court of Appeals
- **Date:** 2019
- **Summary:** County denied conditional use permit for additional structure height at gravel operation. Findings addressed overall operation rather than incremental impact of height request.
- **Outcome:** Kilgore prevailed. Denial was arbitrary and capricious where findings did not address the specific incremental impact of the limited request.
- **Training Value:** Tests specificity requirements for conditional use denials; arbitrary and capricious standard.
- **Source:** [Conditional Uses - Property Rights Ombudsman](https://propertyrights.utah.gov/find-the-law/legal-topics/conditional-uses/)

---

### Provo City v. Ivie
- **Citation:** 2004 UT 30
- **Court:** Utah Supreme Court
- **Date:** 2004
- **Summary:** Addressed scope of municipal authority over property rights.
- **Outcome:** Established that cities may only exercise authority granted to them by the Legislature - principle limiting municipal overreach.
- **Training Value:** Foundational case on Dillon's Rule application in Utah; limits of municipal power.
- **Source:** [Land Use Regulation Cases](https://propertyrights.utah.gov/find-the-law/appellate-decisions/land-use-regulation-cases/)

---

### Bountiful City v. DeLuca
- **Citation:** 826 P.2d 170 (Utah 1992)
- **Court:** Utah Supreme Court
- **Date:** 1992
- **Summary:** Addressed when regulation becomes a compensable taking.
- **Outcome:** Established that regulation becomes a taking "if the regulation deprives owner of a significant amount of economic value."
- **Training Value:** Utah's application of regulatory takings doctrine; threshold for compensation.
- **Source:** [Takings and Eminent Domain](https://propertyrights.utah.gov/takings-and-eminent-domain/)

---

### 2025 UT App 55 - Building Permit Denial
- **Citation:** 2025 UT App 55
- **Court:** Utah Court of Appeals
- **Date:** 2025
- **Summary:** City denied building permit for existing improperly subdivided lot based on off-site road conditions.
- **Outcome:** Property owner prevailed. Court reversed denial because city ordinances allowing development of such lots did not plainly restrict application based on off-site road conditions.
- **Training Value:** Tests limits of conditions cities can impose; plain language interpretation of ordinances.
- **Source:** [Case Summaries](https://propertyrights.utah.gov/case-summaries/)

---

### 2025 UT App 122 - Billboard Relocation Estoppel
- **Citation:** 2025 UT App 122
- **Court:** Utah Court of Appeals
- **Date:** 2025
- **Summary:** Salt Lake City provided written instructions to billboard relocation applicant, then refused compensation upon denial.
- **Outcome:** Applicant prevailed. City was estopped from refusing compensation based on its own prior written instructions.
- **Training Value:** Estoppel doctrine against government; reliance on government representations.
- **Source:** [Case Summaries](https://propertyrights.utah.gov/case-summaries/)

## FEDERAL PRECEDENT CASES (Applicable in Utah)

### Sheetz v. County of El Dorado
- **Citation:** 601 U.S. ___ (2024)
- **Court:** U.S. Supreme Court
- **Date:** 2024
- **Summary:** Whether Nollan/Dolan heightened scrutiny applies to legislatively imposed permit conditions.
- **Outcome:** Property owner prevailed. Unanimous ruling that Takings Clause does not distinguish between legislative and administrative conditions; Nollan/Dolan applies to all exactions.
- **Training Value:** Major expansion of exaction scrutiny; directly applicable to Utah land use.
- **Source:** [Sheetz v. County of El Dorado](https://propertyrights.utah.gov/2024/04/12/new-case-summary-sheetz-v-county-of-el-dorado/)

---

### Kelo v. City of New London
- **Citation:** 545 U.S. 469 (2005)
- **Court:** U.S. Supreme Court
- **Date:** 2005
- **Summary:** Whether economic development constitutes "public use" for eminent domain purposes.
- **Outcome:** City prevailed; taking permitted. But triggered nationwide legislative response, with 47 states strengthening eminent domain protections.
- **Training Value:** Defines federal floor for public use requirement; Utah has additional statutory protections.
- **Source:** [Kelo v. City of New London](https://propertyrights.utah.gov/find-the-law/appellate-decisions/kelo-v-city-of-new-london/)
