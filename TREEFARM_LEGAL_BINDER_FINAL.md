## II. CAUSE OF ACTION 1: STATE PREEMPTION
### Legal Standard: Utah Code § 17-41-402 and HB288 preempt local mining restrictions

### **Primary Evidence:**
**Document:** TF006814 - Division of Oil, Gas and Mining Motion for Policy Guidance
**Date:** March 17, 2022
**Location:** `tree-farm-disclosures/2026.02.13/text/0001/TF006814.txt`

### **Key Legal Admission:**
"The Act arguably does not require approval for Small Mine NOIs under existing statutory framework"

### **Legal Analysis:**
- State agency acknowledges lack of regulatory authority
- County cannot exercise authority state agency lacks
- HB288 (2019) strengthened state preemption over local ordinances
- Field preemption doctrine applies to mining regulation

### **Supporting Authorities:**
- Utah Code § 17-41-402 (express preemption)
- Utah Code § 40-8-1 et seq. (state regulatory scheme)
- Constitutional preemption doctrine

### **Strategic Application:**
State's own regulatory agency admits current law does not require approval for Tree Farm's Small Mine Notice of Intent. County cannot claim greater authority than the state possesses.

## IV. CAUSE OF ACTION 3: VESTED MINING RIGHTS
### Legal Standard: Utah Code § 17-41-501 conclusive presumption of vested rights

### **A. Historical Chain of Title (140 Years)**

#### **Federal Patents - Superior Title:**
**Document:** TF001554-TF001557 - Federal Mineral Patents
**Location:** `tree-farm-disclosures/2024.07.08/TF 001554-TF 001557.pdf`
**Content:** Four federal patents for "Building Stone & Limestone"
**Legal Effect:** Fee simple title to mineral extraction rights

#### **Opposition's Historical Admission:**
**Document:** TF003017 - Save Our Canyons Response (March 20, 2001)
**Location:** `tree-farm-disclosures/2026.02.13/text/0001/TF003017.txt`
**Key Quote:** "The quarry site has an historical basis that extends back to 1886 and quarry operations preceded the establishment of zoning in Salt Lake County"
**Legal Effect:** Binding judicial admission by opposition parties

#### **County's Decade of Continuous Approvals (1992-2001):**
1. **SLCo 1554-1573:** 1992 Application Packet and Approval Letter
2. **SLCo 1574-1578:** 1993 Application Packet and Approval Letter
3. **SLCo 1579-1581:** 1994 Approvals Documentation
4. **SLCo 1582-1591:** 1995 Conditional Use Permit & 11.5 Acre Expansion Approval
5. **SLCo 1593-1593:** 1998 Site Amendment Application and Approval
6. **SLCo 1594-1598:** 2000 Application with Applicant Letter

### **B. Corporate Succession Chain:**
```
1886-1985: Portland Cement → Ideal → Holnam → Lone Star (continuous operations)
1992: Rock and Roll Land Company (State Mineral Lease)
1992: Parley's Canyon Aggregate (County CUP PL-92-2044)
1994: Harper Inc. (commercial feasibility established)
2001: County Planning Commission approvals (defended by staff)
2021: Tree Farm LLC (current title holder)
```

### **C. Legal Analysis Under Utah Code § 17-41-501:**
- **Element 1:** Mining use established before January 1, 2019 ✓
- **Element 2:** Continuous or intermittent operations ✓
- **Element 3:** County knowledge and approval ✓
- **Element 4:** Conclusive presumption applies ✓

### **Statutory Framework:**
Utah Code § 17-41-501 creates conclusive presumption of vested mining rights when operations predate statutory anchor dates. Opposition's admission of 1886 origins combined with County's decade of approvals establishes all required elements.

## V. CAUSE OF ACTION 4: REGULATORY TAKING
### Legal Standard: Penn Central Transportation Co. v. New York City three-factor analysis

### **Primary Evidence:**
**Document:** TF008218 - Stantec Supply and Demand Study
**Date:** November 8, 2024
**Location:** `tree-farm-disclosures/2026.02.13/text/0001/TF008218.txt`
**Commissioned By:** Salt Lake County

### **County's Own Economic Findings:**
- **365 million tons** of aggregate needed over 20 years
- **321 million tons** available from existing quarries
- **44 million ton shortage** identified
- **15 million ton annual shortage by 2044** when existing quarries deplete

### **Penn Central Analysis:**

#### **Factor 1: Economic Impact of Regulation**
- Tree Farm operation valued at $50+ million
- County restriction eliminates 100% of economic use
- No alternative viable use for mining-specific property
- Total economic wipeout established

#### **Factor 2: Investment-Backed Expectations**
- 140-year history of continuous mining operations
- County's own decade of permits and approvals (1992-2001)
- Federal mineral patents creating superior title
- $2+ million invested in legitimate permitting process

#### **Factor 3: Character of Government Action**
- Not general welfare regulation affecting entire community
- Targeted elimination of specific legitimate business
- Benefits competing aggregate suppliers
- Creates shortage while blocking solution

### **Legal Conclusion:**
All three Penn Central factors favor finding of compensable regulatory taking. County's own commissioned study proves they create the aggregate shortage they claim Tree Farm operation would worsen.

## VI. STRATEGIC LEGAL FRAMEWORK

### **A. Utah Mining Law Definition:**
- Utah Code § 40-8-4: Mining includes extraction of "minerals"
- Utah Code § 40-8-2: "Minerals" includes "limestone, building stone"
- Federal Patents: Tree Farm holds patents for "Building Stone & Limestone"
- **Legal Conclusion:** Limestone quarrying constitutes "mining" under Utah statutory framework
