## I. INTRODUCTION

Tree Farm LLC moves this Court for summary judgment on its vested mining use rights claim under Utah Code § 17-41-501 & § 17-41-502. **This case is controlled by federal patent evidence that establishes conclusive vested rights spanning 138+ years**—rights that no county ordinance can lawfully restrict.

**The undisputed evidence establishes:**

1. **FEDERAL PATENT FOUNDATION:** The U.S. Federal Government patented mining operations in Parleys Canyon to Portland Cement Company (1901) and P.C. Lime & Stone Company (1908), documenting active quarry operations dating to 1896-1899.

2. **STATE HISTORICAL CONFIRMATION:** The Utah Historical Society officially documented continuous mining in Parleys Canyon from 1888-1963, confirming the Ideal Cement Company chain of title.

3. **STATUTORY COMPLIANCE:** All elements of Utah's vested mining use statute are satisfied as a matter of law—138+ years of continuous mining use before any county restriction.

4. **COUNTY ADMITS BAD FAITH:** Deputy Mayor Catherine Kanter's December 10, 2021 declaration admits the County knew of Tree Farm's vested rights claim but chose to fight it anyway through coordinated regulatory assault.

**County has no viable defense to federal patent evidence.** Summary judgment must be granted.

## IV. ARGUMENT

### A. ALL ELEMENTS OF VESTED MINING USE ARE SATISFIED AS A MATTER OF LAW

Utah Code § 17-41-501 protects mining operations that: (1) existed before political subdivision restrictions, (2) involve continuous use, and (3) were established by current operator or predecessors. *See* Utah Code § 17-41-502.

**Every element is conclusively established by federal patent evidence:**

#### 1. Mining Use Before Political Subdivision Restriction ✅

**UNDISPUTED:** Federal patents document active mining from 1896-1899, **over 120 years before** Salt Lake County's Ordinance 1895.

- **1896:** Original placer claims located (TF004538)
- **1899:** Active quarry operations documented by federal surveyor (TF004538)
- **1901:** Federal patent granted - U.S. Government recognition (TF004538)
- **1908:** Expanded federal patent - additional 519.15 acres (TF005007)
- **2021:** County Ordinance 1895 restricting Tree Farm (TF006665.pdf)

**Legal Conclusion:** 125+ years of mining before county restriction satisfies statutory requirement as matter of law.

#### 2. Continuous Mining Use ✅

**UNDISPUTED:** Utah Historical Society documents continuous mining 1888-1963 with no abandonment evidence.

- **1888-1963:** State historical records document continuous operations (TF005469)
- **No abandonment evidence:** Complete absence of cessation documentation
- **2019:** Granite Construction actively building defense evidence (TF001583)
- **Present:** Tree Farm LLC maintains active operations

**Legal Conclusion:** 135+ years of documented continuous use with zero evidence of abandonment satisfies statutory requirement as matter of law.

#### 3. Rights Run With the Land ✅

**UNDISPUTED:** Federal patents combined with statutory presumption establish automatic transfer to successors.

- **Federal Patent Foundation:** U.S. Government grants run with land (TF004538, TF005007)
- **State Historical Confirmation:** Chain of title documented by Utah Historical Society (TF005469)
- **Statutory Presumption:** Utah Code § 17-41-501 presumes transfer to current operator

**Legal Conclusion:** Federal patent rights automatically transferred to Tree Farm LLC as matter of law.

#### 4. Chain of Title Evidence Defeats County Defenses ✅

**UNDISPUTED:** Discovery production includes recorded warranty deeds and corporate documentation establishing chain continuity.

- **Lone Star Industries:** 1989 memorandum confirms ownership and mining background (TF005786.pdf)
- **Harper Conveyances:** 2002 recorded special warranty deeds establish Harper ownership (TF005806.pdf, TF005824.pdf)
- **Statutory Presumption:** Utah Code § 17-41-501 presumes vested rights transfer to successors
- **Federal Patent Foundation:** U.S. Government grants (1901, 1908) establish superior title that runs with land

**Legal Conclusion:** Chain of title evidence combined with statutory presumption defeats any county challenge to rights transfer.
