## I. LEGAL FRAMEWORK

### Utah Code § 17-41-501: Vested Mining Use — Conclusive Presumption

**Statutory Definition:**
> "Vested mining use" means a mining use by a mine operator that existed or was conducted before a political subdivision prohibits, restricts, or otherwise limits a mining use.

**Legal Standard:**
- **Conclusively presumed** if mining existed before restriction
- **Burden on COUNTY** to prove by clear and convincing evidence that vested use was NOT established
- **Runs with the land** — transfers to successive owners
- **May be changed** to another mining use without losing vested status

### Utah Code § 17-41-502: Rights of Mine Operator with Vested Mining Use

**Statutory Rights:**
1. Right to expand to any land mine operator owns or controls
2. Right to expand to contiguous new land in same mineral trend
3. Right to increase production, alter methods, mine different minerals
4. Right to discontinue and reactivate without abandonment
5. **Presumption favors expansion** — only "clear and convincing evidence" of imminent endangerment can block

### Supporting Case Law

**Gibbons & Reed Co. v. North Salt Lake City (1967)**
- **Doctrine of Diminishing Assets:** Extractive business may use entire parcel, not just area excavated when ordinance passed
- **Horizontal Expansion Rights:** Mining operation can expand to full parcel boundary
- **Still controlling law in Utah**

**Utah Property Rights Ombudsman Guidance**
- Gravel pits operated by permit or not abandoned for 24 months = **presumed vested**
- **Burden on challenger** to disprove vested rights
- Diminishing assets doctrine allows horizontal expansion to full parcel boundary
